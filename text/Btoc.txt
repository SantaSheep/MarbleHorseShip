===Introduction to B2C===
Business-to-consumer (B2C) is a type of e-commerce transaction that involves the sale of goods and services from a business to an individual consumer. B2C transactions are typically conducted online, but can also take place in physical stores. B2C is a subset of e-commerce, which is the buying and selling of goods and services over the internet. B2C is one of the most popular forms of e-commerce, as it allows businesses to reach a large number of potential customers quickly and easily.

===Benefits of B2C===
B2C transactions offer a number of benefits to businesses and consumers alike. For businesses, B2C transactions allow them to reach a larger customer base, as well as reduce costs associated with traditional retail stores. Additionally, B2C transactions allow businesses to track customer data, which can be used to improve [[Customer Service|customer service]] and [[Target Market|target market]]ing efforts. For consumers, B2C transactions offer convenience, as they can shop from the comfort of their own home. Additionally, B2C transactions often offer lower prices than traditional retail stores, as businesses are able to reduce costs associated with physical stores.
===Challenges of B2C===
Despite the numerous benefits of B2C transactions, there are also a number of challenges associated with them. For businesses, the biggest challenge is ensuring customer satisfaction. As B2C transactions are conducted online, businesses must ensure that their websites are secure and easy to use. Additionally, businesses must ensure that their customer service is up to par, as customers may not be able to physically visit a store if they have an issue. For consumers, the biggest challenge is ensuring that the goods and services they purchase are of good quality. As B2C transactions are conducted online, consumers must be sure to research the products they are purchasing to ensure that they are of good quality.
