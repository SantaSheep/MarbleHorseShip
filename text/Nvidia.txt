===Introduction to Nvidia===

Nvidia is a global leader in visual computing technologies and the inventor of the GPU, or graphics processing unit. Founded in 1993, Nvidia has become one of the most successful companies in the technology industry, with a [[Market Capitalization|market capitalization]] of over $100 billion. Nvidia designs and manufactures graphics cards, processors, and other components for the gaming, professional, and consumer markets. The company also provides software and services for gaming, [[Cloud Computing|cloud computing]], and artificial intelligence. Nvidia is headquartered in Santa Clara, California, and has offices in more than 20 countries around the world.
===History of Nvidia===

Nvidia was founded in 1993 by Jen-Hsun Huang, Curtis Priem, and Chris Malachowsky. The company was initially focused on developing graphics chips for the PC market. In 1999, Nvidia released the GeForce 256, the world’s first GPU, which revolutionized the gaming industry. Since then, Nvidia has continued to innovate and develop new products and technologies, such as the CUDA platform for parallel computing, the Tegra mobile processor, and the Tesla line of GPUs for high-performance computing.
===Products and Services Offered by Nvidia===

Nvidia offers a wide range of products and services for the gaming, professional, and consumer markets. The company’s gaming products include graphics cards, processors, and other components for PC gaming. Nvidia also offers software and services for gaming, such as the GeForce Experience, which allows gamers to optimize their gaming experience. For the professional market, Nvidia offers the Quadro line of professional graphics cards, as well as the Tesla line of GPUs for high-performance computing. For the consumer market, Nvidia offers the Tegra mobile processor, which is used in smartphones, tablets, and other mobile devices.
===Financial Performance of Nvidia===

Nvidia has experienced tremendous growth in recent years, with revenue increasing from $4.7 billion in 2017 to $11.7 billion in 2020. The company’s [[Net Income|net income]] has also increased significantly, from $1.2 billion in 2017 to $3.2 billion in 2020. Nvidia’s stock price has also increased significantly, from $90.34 in 2017 to $541.90 in 2021.
