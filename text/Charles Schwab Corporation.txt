===Introduction to Charles Schwab Corporation===

Charles Schwab Corporation is a financial services company based in San Francisco, California. It is one of the largest brokerage firms in the United States and provides a wide range of services to its customers, including investment advice, brokerage services, banking services, and retirement planning. The company was founded in 1971 by Charles Schwab and has grown to become one of the most successful financial services companies in the world.
===History of Charles Schwab Corporation===

Charles Schwab founded the company in 1971 with the goal of providing low-cost brokerage services to individual investors. The company quickly grew and by 1975, it had become the largest discount broker in the United States. In the 1980s, the company expanded its services to include banking and retirement planning. In the 1990s, the company began offering online trading services and in 2000, it acquired U.S. Trust Corporation, making it one of the largest [[Wealth Management|wealth management]] firms in the United States.
===Business Model of Charles Schwab Corporation===

Charles Schwab Corporation operates a full-service brokerage business, offering a wide range of services to its customers. These services include investment advice, brokerage services, banking services, and retirement planning. The company also offers online trading services, allowing customers to trade stocks, bonds, [[Mutual Fund|mutual fund]]s, and other [[Financial Instrument|financial instrument]]s. The company earns revenue from commissions, fees, and other charges associated with its services.
===Financial Performance of Charles Schwab Corporation===

Charles Schwab Corporation has been consistently profitable since its founding in 1971. In 2019, the company reported total revenue of $14.2 billion and [[Net Income|net income]] of $3.3 billion.
