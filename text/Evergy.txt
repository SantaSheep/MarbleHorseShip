===Introduction to Evergy===

Evergy is a leading energy provider in the United States, serving more than 1.6 million customers in Kansas and Missouri. The company is a combination of two former utilities, Kansas City Power & Light and KCP& Greater Missouri Operations, and is headquartered in Kansas City, Missouri. Evergy is a publicly traded company on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) under the ticker symbol EVRG. The company is a member of the S&P 500 and is a component of the Dow Jones Utility Average.
===History of Evergy===

Evergy was formed in 2018 when Kansas City Power & Light and KCP& Greater Missouri Operations merged. The merger created the largest utility in the state of Missouri and the second largest in Kansas. The merger was approved by the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) and the Missouri Public Service Commission (MPSC). The merger was completed in April 2018 and the company began trading on the NYSE in May 2018.
===Business Model of Evergy===

Evergy is a regulated utility that provides electricity and natural gas to customers in Kansas and Missouri. The company operates a network of power plants, transmission lines, and distribution systems to deliver electricity and natural gas to its customers. Evergy also provides energy efficiency programs and renewable energy options to its customers.
