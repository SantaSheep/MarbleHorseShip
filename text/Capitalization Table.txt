===Introduction to Capitalization Tables === 
A capitalization table, also known as a cap table, is a document that outlines the ownership structure of a company. It is used to track the equity ownership of a company, including the number of shares outstanding, the percentage of ownership held by each shareholder, and the value of each share. The cap table is an important tool for entrepreneurs, investors, and other stakeholders in a company, as it provides a clear picture of the company’s ownership structure.
===Types of Capitalization Tables === 
There are two main types of capitalization tables: pre-money and post-money. Pre-money cap tables are used to track the ownership structure of a company before any outside investments have been made. Post-money cap tables are used to track the ownership structure of a company after outside investments have been made.
===Benefits of Capitalization Tables === 
Capitalization tables provide a number of benefits to entrepreneurs, investors, and other stakeholders in a company. They provide a clear picture of the company’s ownership structure, which can be used to make informed decisions about the company’s future. They also provide a way to track the value of each share, which can be used to calculate the value of the company. Finally, they provide a way to track the percentage of ownership held by each shareholder, which can be used to ensure that all shareholders are treated fairly.
===How to Create a Capitalization Table === 
Creating a capitalization table is relatively straightforward. The first step is to determine the number of shares outstanding. This can be done by looking at the company’s articles of incorporation or other legal documents. Once the number of shares outstanding is determined, the next step is to determine the percentage of ownership held by each shareholder. This can be done by looking at the company’s stock certificates or other legal documents. Finally, the value of each share can be determined by looking at the company’s financial statements or other documents.
===Common Mistakes to Avoid === 
When creating a capitalization table, it is important to avoid common mistakes. One of the most common mistakes is not accounting for all of the shares outstanding.
