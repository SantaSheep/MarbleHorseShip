===Introduction to Electronic Arts===

Electronic Arts (EA) is a leading global interactive entertainment software company. Founded in 1982, EA develops, publishes, and distributes interactive software worldwide for video game consoles, personal computers, mobile phones, and the Internet. EA is headquartered in Redwood City, California, and has offices in more than 25 countries around the world. EA is one of the largest video game publishers in the world, with a portfolio of popular franchises such as FIFA, Madden NFL, The Sims, Battlefield, and Need for Speed.
===History of Electronic Arts===

EA was founded in 1982 by Trip Hawkins, who had previously worked at Apple. Hawkins wanted to create a company that focused on creating and publishing computer games. EA was the first company to develop and publish computer games for the Apple  computer. In the early years, EA focused on creating and publishing computer games for the Apple , Commodore 64, and IBM PC.

In the late 1980s, EA began to expand its business model to include console games. EA was one of the first companies to develop and publish games for the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES). EA also developed and published games for the Sega Genesis and Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES).

In the 1990s, EA continued to expand its business model to include online gaming. EA was one of the first companies to develop and publish online games, such as Ultima Online and The Sims Online. EA also developed and published games for the Sony PlayStation and Microsoft Xbox.
===Business Model of Electronic Arts===

EA’s business model is based on creating and publishing interactive entertainment software. EA develops and publishes games for video game consoles, personal computers, mobile phones, and the Internet. EA also licenses its intellectual property to third-party developers and publishers. EA also provides digital distribution services for its own games and third-party games.
===Financial Performance of Electronic Arts===

EA has been profitable since its inception. In [[Fiscal Year|fiscal year]] 2020, EA reported total revenue of $5.5 billion, up 8% from the prior year. EA’s [[Net Income|net income]] was $1.2 billion, up 11% from the prior year. EA’s [[Operating Margin|operating margin]] was 22.3%, up from 21.3% in the prior year.
===Recent Developments at Electronic Arts===

In recent years, EA has been focused on expanding its digital business. EA has acquired several companies to expand its digital offerings, including Glu Mobile, Playfish, and Respawn Entertainment. EA has also launched several new digital services, such as EA Access and Origin Access.
