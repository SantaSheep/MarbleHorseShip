===Introduction to Costco===
Costco Wholesale Corporation is an American [[Multinational Corporation|multinational corporation]] that operates a chain of membership-only warehouse clubs. It is the second largest retailer in the world after Walmart, and the world’s largest membership warehouse club chain. Costco is headquartered in Issaquah, Washington, and operates 785 warehouses worldwide, including 527 in the United States and Puerto Rico, 100 in Canada, 39 in Mexico, 28 in the United Kingdom, 26 in Japan, 16 in Korea, 13 in Taiwan, 10 in Australia, two in Spain, one in Iceland, and one in France.
===History of Costco===
Costco was founded in 1983 by James Sinegal and Jeffrey Brotman. The first warehouse opened in Seattle, Washington, and the company quickly grew to become one of the largest retailers in the United States. In 1993, Costco merged with Price Club, a rival warehouse club, and the combined company was renamed Costco Companies, Inc. In 1997, the company went public and changed its name to Costco Wholesale Corporation.
===Business Model of Costco===
Costco’s business model is based on the concept of bulk buying. The company offers a wide variety of products at discounted prices, and customers can purchase items in bulk to save even more money. Costco also offers a variety of services, such as optical, pharmacy, and travel services. The company also offers a variety of membership options, including a basic membership, an executive membership, and a business membership.
