===Introduction to Regency Centers===

Regency Centers is a [[Real Estate|real estate]] investment trust (REIT) that specializes in the ownership, operation, and development of grocery-anchored shopping centers. Founded in 1963, Regency Centers is one of the oldest and largest REITs in the United States. The company is headquartered in Jacksonville, Florida and is publicly traded on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol REG. Regency Centers owns and operates over 300 shopping centers in the United States, with a total of over 24 million square feet of retail space.
===History of Regency Centers===

Regency Centers was founded in 1963 by two real estate entrepreneurs, John  Rood and Robert  White. The company was initially focused on the development of shopping centers in the southeastern United States. Over the years, Regency Centers has grown to become one of the largest and most successful REITs in the United States. The company has expanded its portfolio to include shopping centers in over 30 states, with a focus on grocery-anchored shopping centers.
