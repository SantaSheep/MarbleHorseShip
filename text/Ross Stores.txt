===Introduction to Ross Stores===

Ross Stores, Inc. is an American chain of off-price department stores headquartered in Dublin, California. It is the second-largest off-price retailer in the United States, behind only [[Tjx Companies|TJX Companies]]. Ross Stores operates 1,400 stores in 39 states, the District of Columbia, and Guam, as well as an e-commerce website. The company offers a wide variety of apparel, accessories, footwear, home décor, and other products at discounted prices. Ross Stores is a publicly traded company listed on the NASDAQ stock exchange under the ticker symbol ROST.
===History of Ross Stores===

Ross Stores was founded in 1957 by Morris Ross and his wife, Barbara. The couple opened their first store in San Bruno, California, and quickly expanded to other locations in the Bay Area. By the 1970s, Ross Stores had grown to more than 100 stores in California and other western states. In 1982, the company went public and began to expand rapidly. By the 1990s, Ross Stores had become a national chain with stores in nearly every state. Today, Ross Stores is one of the largest off-price retailers in the United States.
