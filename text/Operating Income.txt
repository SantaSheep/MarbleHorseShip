===Introduction to Operating Income === 
Operating income is a measure of a company’s financial performance that is used to assess the profitability of its core operations. It is calculated by subtracting operating expenses from total revenue. Operating income is also known as operating profit, earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT), or operating earnings. It is an important metric for investors and analysts to consider when evaluating a company’s financial health.
===Definition of Operating Income === 
Operating income is a measure of a company’s profitability that is calculated by subtracting operating expenses from total revenue. Operating expenses include the costs of labor, materials, and other expenses associated with running the business. Operating income does not include non-operating items such as interest income, taxes, or gains and losses from investments.
===Calculating Operating Income === 
Operating income is calculated by subtracting operating expenses from total revenue. Operating expenses include the costs of labor, materials, and other expenses associated with running the business. Operating income does not include non-operating items such as interest income, taxes, or gains and losses from investments.
===Benefits of Operating Income === 
Operating income is an important metric for investors and analysts to consider when evaluating a company’s financial health. It provides a measure of the company’s profitability that is not affected by non-operating items such as taxes, interest income, or gains and losses from investments. Operating income is also a useful metric for comparing the performance of different companies in the same industry.
===Examples of Operating Income === 
An example of operating income is a company that has total revenue of $100,000 and operating expenses of $80,000. The company’s operating income would be $20,000. Another example is a company that has total revenue of $200,000 and operating expenses of $150,000. The company’s operating income would be $50,000.

Operating income is an important measure of a company’s financial performance that is used to assess the profitability of its core operations.
