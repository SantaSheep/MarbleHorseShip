===Introduction to American International Group===

American International Group, Inc. (AIG) is a global insurance and financial services company headquartered in New York City. Founded in 1919, AIG is one of the world’s largest insurance companies, with operations in more than 80 countries and jurisdictions. AIG provides a wide range of insurance and financial services, including [[Life Insurance|life insurance]], property and casualty insurance, retirement services, and [[Asset Management|asset management]]. AIG also provides a variety of financial services, such as [[Investment Banking|investment banking]], securities trading, and [[Wealth Management|wealth management]].
===History of American International Group===

AIG was founded in 1919 by Cornelius Vander Starr, an American entrepreneur who had previously worked in the insurance industry in China. Starr established AIG as a [[Holding Company|holding company]] for a number of insurance companies he had acquired in the United States and abroad. In the early years, AIG focused on providing life insurance and property and casualty insurance to individuals and businesses.

In the 1950s, AIG began to expand its operations into other areas of the financial services industry. In 1957, AIG acquired the investment banking firm Loeb, Rhoades & Co., which allowed AIG to offer a full range of financial services, including investment banking, securities trading, and wealth management. In the 1960s, AIG began to expand its operations into other countries, and by the 1970s, AIG had become a global insurance and financial services company.
