===Introduction to Activity Ratios === 
Activity ratios are a type of financial ratio used to measure a company’s efficiency and performance. They are used to measure how quickly a company is able to convert its assets into cash or sales. Activity ratios are also known as turnover ratios or asset management ratios. They are used to measure the liquidity, efficiency, and profitability of a company.
===Examples of Activity Ratios === 
The most common activity ratios are inventory turnover, accounts receivable turnover, and accounts payable turnover. These ratios measure how quickly a company is able to convert its assets into cash or sales.
===Inventory Turnover === 
Inventory turnover is a measure of how quickly a company is able to sell its inventory. It is calculated by dividing the cost of goods sold by the average inventory. A higher inventory turnover ratio indicates that a company is able to sell its inventory quickly and efficiently.
===Accounts Receivable Turnover === 
Accounts receivable turnover is a measure of how quickly a company is able to collect payments from its customers. It is calculated by dividing the total sales by the average accounts receivable. A higher accounts receivable turnover ratio indicates that a company is able to collect payments quickly and efficiently.
===Accounts Payable Turnover === 
Accounts payable turnover is a measure of how quickly a company is able to pay its suppliers. It is calculated by dividing the total purchases by the average accounts payable. A higher accounts payable turnover ratio indicates that a company is able to pay its suppliers quickly and efficiently.
===Benefits of Activity Ratios === 
Activity ratios provide a quick and easy way to measure a company’s efficiency and performance. They can be used to compare a company’s performance to that of its competitors. Activity ratios can also be used to identify areas of improvement and to set goals for the future.
===Challenges of Activity Ratios === 
Activity ratios can be difficult to interpret and can be affected by a variety of factors. For example, a company’s inventory turnover ratio may be affected by the type of products it sells, the pricing of its products, and the availability of its products.
===How to Calculate Activity Ratios === 
Activity ratios are calculated by dividing one number by another. For example, the inventory turnover ratio is calculated by dividing the cost of goods sold by the average inventory. The accounts receivable turnover ratio is calculated by dividing the total sales by the average accounts receivable. The accounts payable turnover ratio is calculated by dividing the total purchases by the average accounts payable.
