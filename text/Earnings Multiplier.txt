===Introduction to Earnings Multiplier===

Earnings multiplier is a financial term used to measure the profitability of a company. It is a ratio that compares the company’s earnings to its total assets. The earnings multiplier is used to determine the [[Return On Investment|return on investment]] (ROI) of a company and to compare the profitability of different companies. It is also used to assess the financial health of a company and to make investment decisions.
===Definition of Earnings Multiplier===

The earnings multiplier is a financial ratio that measures the profitability of a company. It is calculated by dividing the company’s earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT) by its total assets. The higher the earnings multiplier, the more profitable the company is.

===Calculating Earnings Multiplier===

The earnings multiplier is calculated by dividing the company’s earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT) by its total assets. The formula for calculating the earnings multiplier is:

Earnings Multiplier = EBIT / Total Assets
