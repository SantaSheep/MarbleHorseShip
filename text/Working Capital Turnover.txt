===Introduction to Working Capital Turnover===

[[Working Capital|Working capital]] turnover is a financial ratio that measures the efficiency of a company’s use of its working capital. It is used to assess the liquidity of a company and its ability to generate [[Cash Flow|cash flow]] from its operations. Working capital turnover is an important indicator of a company’s financial health and can be used to compare the performance of different companies in the same industry.
===Definition of Working Capital Turnover===

Working capital turnover is a financial ratio that measures the efficiency of a company’s use of its working capital. It is calculated by dividing the company’s [[Net Sales|net sales]] by its average working capital. The ratio is expressed as a percentage and is used to assess the liquidity of a company and its ability to generate cash flow from its operations.
===Components of Working Capital Turnover===

Working capital turnover is calculated by dividing the company’s net sales by its average working capital. The components of working capital turnover include the company’s net sales, average working capital, and the ratio expressed as a percentage. Net sales are the total amount of money a company earns from the sale of its products or services. Average working capital is the average amount of money a company has available to cover its short-term liabilities.
===Calculating Working Capital Turnover===

Working capital turnover is calculated by dividing the company’s net sales by its average working capital. The formula for calculating working capital turnover is: Working Capital Turnover = Net Sales / Average Working Capital. For example, if a company has net sales of $1,000,000 and average working capital of $500,000, then the working capital turnover would be 2.0 (1,000,000/500,000).
===Benefits of Working Capital Turnover===

Working capital turnover is an important indicator of a company’s financial health and can be used to compare the performance of different companies in the same industry. A higher working capital turnover ratio indicates that a company is more efficient in its use of working capital and is better able to generate cash flow from its operations. A lower working capital turnover ratio indicates that a company is less efficient in its use of working capital and is less able to generate cash flow from its operations.
===Limitations of Working Capital Turnover===

Although working capital turnover is a useful indicator of a company’s financial health, it is important to note that it does not take into account other factors such as the company’s debt levels or the quality of its assets. Additionally, working capital turnover does not take into account the company’s ability to generate profits from its operations.
