===Introduction to the 80/20 Rule===

The 80/20 Rule, also known as the [[Pareto Principle|Pareto Principle]], is a concept that states that 80% of the effects come from 20% of the causes. This rule is used in many different fields, including economics, business, and finance. It is based on the observation that a small number of inputs are responsible for the majority of outputs. The 80/20 Rule is a useful tool for understanding how to maximize efficiency and productivity in any given situation.
===History of the 80/20 Rule===

The 80/20 Rule was first proposed by Italian economist Vilfredo Pareto in the late 19th century. Pareto observed that 80% of the land in Italy was owned by 20% of the population. He then developed the concept of the 80/20 Rule, which states that 80% of the effects come from 20% of the causes. This concept has since been applied to many different fields, including economics, business, and finance.
