===Introduction to the Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) Act of 2002 === 
The Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002, commonly referred to as SOX, is a federal law that was enacted in response to the corporate scandals of the early 2000s. The law was designed to protect investors from fraudulent financial reporting by publicly traded companies. SOX requires companies to maintain accurate financial records and to adhere to certain standards of corporate governance. The law also established the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board (PCAOB) to oversee the auditing of public companies.

Companies are now required to maintain accurate financial records and to adhere to certain standards of corporate governance.
