===Introduction to Sampling in Finance - Overview of the concept and its importance=== 
Sampling is a powerful tool used in finance to analyze large datasets and draw meaningful conclusions. It is a process of selecting a subset of data from a larger population to represent the entire population. Sampling is used in finance to identify trends, make predictions, and assess risk. It is an important tool for financial professionals, as it allows them to make decisions quickly and accurately.
===Types of Sampling - Examples of different sampling techniques=== 
There are several different types of sampling techniques used in finance. These include simple random sampling, stratified sampling, cluster sampling, systematic sampling, and convenience sampling. Each of these techniques has its own advantages and disadvantages, and it is important to understand the differences between them in order to choose the best method for a given situation.
