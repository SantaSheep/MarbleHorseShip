===Introduction to Impairment===
Impairment is an accounting term used to describe the decrease in the value of an asset due to a variety of factors. Impairment can be caused by a variety of factors, including changes in market conditions, technological advances, or changes in the assetâ€™s use. Impairment is a non-cash expense, meaning that it does not involve the exchange of cash. Instead, it is a decrease in the value of an asset that is recorded on the [[Balance Sheet|balance sheet]].
===Accounting Standards for Impairment===
The [[Accounting Standard|accounting standard]]s for impairment are set by the [[Financial Accounting|Financial Accounting]] Standards Board (FASB). The FASB has established a set of rules and guidelines for recognizing and measuring impairment losses. These rules and guidelines are designed to ensure that companies accurately report the value of their assets.
===Impairment Testing===
Impairmen[[T Test|t test]]ing is the process of determining whether an asset has been impaired. This process involves comparing the carrying value of the asset to its [[Fair Value|fair value]]. If the carrying value of the asset is greater than its fair value, then the asset is considered to be impaired.
===Impairment Losses===
When an asset is impaired, the company must record an impairment loss. This loss is recorded as a non-cash expense on the [[Income Statement|income statement]]. The amount of the impairment loss is equal to the difference between the carrying value of the asset and its fair value.
