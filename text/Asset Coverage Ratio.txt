===Introduction to Asset Coverage Ratio === 
Asset Coverage Ratio is a financial metric used to measure the ability of a company to meet its financial obligations. It is a measure of the company’s ability to pay off its debt with its assets. It is an important indicator of a company’s financial health and is used by investors and creditors to assess the risk of investing in or lending to a company.
===What is Asset Coverage Ratio? === 
Asset Coverage Ratio is a measure of a company’s ability to pay off its debt with its assets. It is calculated by dividing the total value of a company’s assets by the total amount of its liabilities. The higher the ratio, the better the company’s ability to pay off its debt.
