===Introduction to TER===

TER, or Total [[Expense Ratio|Expense Ratio]], is a measure of the total costs associated with investing in a [[Mutual Fund|mutual fund]] or exchange-traded fund (ETF). It is expressed as a percentage of the fund’s total assets and is used to compare the costs of different funds. The TER is an important factor to consider when selecting a fund, as it can have a significant impact on the performance of the fund over time.
===Definition of TER===

The Total Expense Ratio (TER) is a measure of the total costs associated with investing in a mutual fund or ETF. It is expressed as a percentage of the fund’s total assets and is used to compare the costs of different funds. The TER includes all of the costs associated with managing and operating the fund, such as management fees, administrative fees, and other expenses.
===Factors Affecting TER===

The TER of a fund is affected by a number of factors, including the size of the fund, the type of investments it holds, and the fees charged by the fund manager. The larger the fund, the lower the TER, as the costs associated with managing the fund are spread out over a larger asset base. The type of investments held by the fund can also affect the TER, as some investments may be more expensive to manage than others. Finally, the fees charged by the fund manager can also have an impact on the TER.
===Calculating TER===

The TER of a fund is calculated by dividing the total expenses associated with the fund by the total assets of the fund. The resulting figure is then expressed as a percentage. For example, if a fund has total expenses of $100,000 and total assets of $1,000,000, the TER would be 10%.
===Examples of TER===

The TER of a fund can vary significantly from one fund to another. For example, a fund that invests in large-cap stocks may have a TER of 0.5%, while a fund that invests in small-cap stocks may have a TER of 1.5%. Similarly, a fund that invests in international stocks may have a TER of 1.0%, while a fund that invests in emerging markets may have a TER of 2.0%.
