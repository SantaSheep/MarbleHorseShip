===Introduction to Written-Down Value === 
Written-down value is a method of accounting used to determine the value of an asset over its useful life. It is a form of depreciation that is used to calculate the value of an asset after it has been used for a certain period of time. This method of accounting is used to determine the value of an asset for tax purposes and to help businesses make decisions about their investments.
===Definition of Written-Down Value === 
Written-down value is the value of an asset after it has been used for a certain period of time. This value is determined by subtracting the amount of depreciation from the original cost of the asset. The amount of depreciation is determined by the useful life of the asset and the rate of depreciation.
===Calculating Written-Down Value ===

The rate of depreciation is the rate at which the value of the asset decreases over time.
