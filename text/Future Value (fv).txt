===Introduction to Future Value (FV) === 
Future Value (FV) is a concept used in finance to calculate the value of an asset at a future date. It is used to determine the present value of an investment, taking into account the time value of money and the rate of return. FV is an important concept for investors, as it helps them to make informed decisions about their investments.
===Calculating Future Value === 
The formula for calculating future value is: FV = PV (1 + r)^n, where PV is the present value, r is the rate of return, and n is the number of periods. The rate of return is the rate of interest earned on the investment, and the number of periods is the length of time the investment will be held.
===Compounding Interest === 
Compounding interest is an important factor in calculating future value. Compounding interest is when the interest earned on an investment is reinvested, and the interest earned on the reinvested amount is also reinvested. This process continues over time, resulting in a higher rate of return than if the interest was not reinvested.
===Factors Affecting Future Value === 
There are several factors that can affect the future value of an investment. These include the rate of return, the length of time the investment is held, inflation, taxes, and fees.
