===Introduction to Internal Rate of Return (IRR)===

Internal [[Rate Of Return|Rate of Return]] (IRR) is a financial metric used to measure the profitability of an investment. It is a [[Discount Rate|discount rate]] that makes the net [[Present Value|present value]] (NPV) of all [[Cash Flow|cash flow]]s from a project or investment equal to zero. IRR is used to compare the profitability of different investments and to determine the maximum rate of return that a project can generate. It is a popular metric among investors and financial analysts as it provides a simple and straightforward way to compare investments.
===Calculating IRR===

The IRR is calculated by finding the discount rate that makes the NPV of all cash flows from a project or investment equal to zero. This is done by trial and error, or by using a financial calculator or spreadsheet software. The formula for calculating IRR is as follows:

IRR = (1 + r)^n - 1

Where r is the discount rate and n is the number of periods.
===Advantages and Disadvantages of IRR===

The main advantage of using IRR is that it provides a simple and straightforward way to compare investments. It is also useful for evaluating projects with different cash flows over time, as it takes into account the [[Time Value Of Money|time value of money]]. However, there are some drawbacks to using IRR. For example, it does not take into account the risk associated with an investment, and it can be difficult to calculate in some cases.
