===Introduction to Tractor Supply===

Tractor Supply Company (TSC) is a publicly traded retail chain of stores that specializes in providing products for home improvement, agriculture, lawn and garden maintenance, and livestock, equine, and pet care. Founded in 1938, TSC is headquartered in Brentwood, Tennessee and operates more than 1,800 stores in 49 states. TSC is the largest rural lifestyle retailer in the United States and is the leader in the retail sale of products for home improvement, agriculture, lawn and garden maintenance, and livestock, equine, and pet care.
===History of Tractor Supply===

Tractor Supply was founded in 1938 by Charles E. Schmidt, Sr. in Minot, North Dakota. The company began as a mail order catalog business, selling tractor parts and supplies to farmers. In 1939, the company opened its first retail store in Minot, North Dakota. Over the next several decades, TSC expanded its retail presence, opening stores in several states. In the late 1990s, TSC began to expand rapidly, opening stores in new markets and acquiring other retail chains. By the early 2000s, TSC had become the largest rural lifestyle retailer in the United States.
