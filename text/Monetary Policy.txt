===Introduction to Monetary Policy===

Monetary policy is the process by which a central bank or other monetary authority manages the [[Money Supply|money supply]] and [[Interest Rate|interest rate]]s in an economy. It is the process by which a government, central bank, or monetary authority manages the money supply and interest rates to achieve specific economic objectives. It is the process of controlling the money supply and interest rates in order to influence economic activity. The primary objective of monetary policy is to maintain price stability and promote [[Economic Growth|economic growth]].
===Types of Monetary Policy===

There are two main types of monetary policy: expansionary and contractionary. Expansionary monetary policy is when the central bank increases the money supply and lowers interest rates in order to stimulate economic activity. Contractionary monetary policy is when the central bank decreases the money supply and raises interest rates in order to slow economic activity.
===Goals of Monetary Policy===

The primary goal of monetary policy is to maintain price stability and promote economic growth. Price stability is achieved when the rate of inflation is low and stable. Economic growth is achieved when the economy is able to produce more goods and services. Other goals of monetary policy include reducing unemployment, promoting financial stability, and maintaining a low and stable [[Exchange Rate|exchange rate]].
===Instruments of Monetary Policy===

The primary instruments of monetary policy are [[Open Market Operations|open market operations]], reserve requirements, and the [[Discount Rate|discount rate]]. Open market operations involve the buying and selling of government securities in order to influence the money supply and interest rates. Reserve requirements are the amount of funds that banks must keep on hand in order to meet their obligations. The discount rate is the rate at which the central bank lends money to [[Commercial Bank|commercial bank]]s.
===Impact of Monetary Policy===

Monetary policy has a significant impact on the economy. Expansionary monetary policy can lead to increased economic activity, higher inflation, and higher interest rates. Contractionary monetary policy can lead to decreased economic activity, lower inflation, and lower interest rates.
===Conclusion===

Monetary policy is the process by which a central bank or other monetary authority manages the money supply and interest rates in an economy. The primary objective of monetary policy is to maintain price stability and promote economic growth. The primary instruments of monetary policy are open market operations, reserve requirements, and the discount rate.
