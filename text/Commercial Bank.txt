===Introduction to Commercial Banking === 
Commercial banking is a type of financial institution that provides services such as accepting deposits, making loans, and providing other financial services to businesses and individuals. Commercial banks are regulated by the government and are typically the most accessible type of financial institution for individuals and businesses. Commercial banks are typically the most accessible type of financial institution for individuals and businesses.
===Types of Commercial Banks === 
Commercial banks can be divided into two main categories: retail banks and investment banks. Retail banks provide services such as checking and savings accounts, loans, and other financial services to individuals and businesses. Investment banks provide services such as underwriting securities, providing advice on mergers and acquisitions, and providing other financial services to corporations and governments.
===Services Offered by Commercial Banks === 
Commercial banks offer a variety of services to their customers. These services include accepting deposits, making loans, providing financial advice, and providing other financial services. Commercial banks also offer services such as online banking, mobile banking, and ATM services.
===Regulations and Compliance === 
Commercial banks are subject to a variety of regulations and compliance requirements. These regulations and compliance requirements are designed to ensure that commercial banks operate in a safe and sound manner and protect the interests of their customers.
===Benefits of Commercial Banking === 
Commercial banking provides a variety of benefits to individuals and businesses. These benefits include access to capital, convenience, and security. Commercial banks also provide a variety of services such as online banking, mobile banking, and ATM services.
===Example of a Commercial Bank Transaction=== 
An example of a commercial bank transaction is a loan. A loan is a type of financial transaction in which a bank lends money to an individual or business. The borrower agrees to repay the loan, with interest, over a specified period of time.
