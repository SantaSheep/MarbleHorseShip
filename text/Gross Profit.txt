===Introduction to Gross Profit === 
Gross profit is a measure of profitability that looks at the difference between a company’s total revenue and its cost of goods sold (COGS). It is a key metric used to assess a company’s financial performance and is often used to compare the performance of different companies in the same industry. Gross profit is also used to calculate other important financial ratios, such as gross margin and return on investment.
===Calculating Gross Profit === 
Gross profit is calculated by subtracting the cost of goods sold (COGS) from total revenue. COGS includes the cost of materials, labor, and overhead associated with producing a product or service.
