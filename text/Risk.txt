===Introduction to Risk===

Risk is an inherent part of any financial decision. It is the potential for loss or gain associated with any investment or financial decision. Risk is an important factor to consider when making any financial decision, as it can have a significant impact on the outcome of the decision. Risk can be divided into two main categories: systematic risk and unsystematic risk. Systematic risk is the risk associated with the entire market, while unsystematic risk is the risk associated with a specific company or industry.
===Types of Risk===

There are several different types of risk that can be associated with any financial decision. These include market risk, [[Credit Risk|credit risk]], [[Liquidity Risk|liquidity risk]], operational risk, and legal risk. Market risk is the risk associated with changes in the overall market, such as changes in [[Interest Rate|interest rate]]s or stock prices. Operational risk is the risk associated with the operations of a company, such as the risk of a data breach or a natural disaster. Legal risk is the risk associated with the legal environment in which a company operates, such as the risk of being sued.
===Risk Management Strategies===

[[Risk Management|Risk management]] is the process of identifying, assessing, and managing risk. Risk management strategies can be divided into two main categories: proactive and reactive. Proactive risk management strategies involve taking steps to reduce the likelihood of a risk occurring, such as diversifying investments or purchasing insurance. Reactive risk management strategies involve taking steps to mitigate the impact of a risk that has already occurred, such as setting up a contingency plan or taking legal action.
===Risk Measurement Techniques===

Risk measurement is the process of quantifying the potential impact of a risk. There are several different techniques that can be used to measure risk, including value at risk (VaR), expected shortfall (ES), and stress testing. VaR is a statistical measure of the maximum potential loss that could occur over a given period of time. ES is a measure of the expected loss that could occur over a given period of time. Stress testing is a simulation of a worst-case scenario to determine the potential impact of a risk.
