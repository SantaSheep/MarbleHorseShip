===Introduction to Internal Auditor===

An [[Internal Audit|internal audit]]or is a professional who is responsible for providing independent and objective assurance to an organization’s management and [[Board Of Directors|board of directors]]. Internal auditors are employed by organizations to evaluate and improve the effectiveness of [[Risk Management|risk management]], control, and governance processes. They are also responsible for providing assurance that the organization’s [[Financial Statements|financial statements]] are accurate and reliable. Internal auditors are expected to be knowledgeable about the organization’s operations, processes, and procedures, and to be able to identify areas of risk and potential fraud.
===Role of Internal Auditor===

The role of an internal auditor is to provide assurance to an organization’s management and board of directors that the organization’s financial statements are accurate and reliable. Internal auditors are also responsible for evaluating and improving the effectiveness of risk management, control, and governance processes. They are expected to identify areas of risk and potential fraud, and to provide recommendations for improvement. Internal auditors are also responsible for providing assurance that the organization’s financial statements are in compliance with applicable laws and regulations.
===Qualifications of Internal Auditor===

Internal auditors must possess a variety of qualifications in order to be successful in their role. They must have a strong understanding of [[Accounting Principles|accounting principles]] and financial reporting standards. They must also have a thorough knowledge of the organization’s operations, processes, and procedures. Additionally, internal auditors must possess strong analytical and problem-solving skills, as well as excellent communication and [[Interpersonal Skills|interpersonal skills]].
