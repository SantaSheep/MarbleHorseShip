===Introduction to General Mills===
General Mills is a multinational food manufacturing company based in Minneapolis, Minnesota. Founded in 1928, the company has grown to become one of the largest food companies in the world, with operations in more than 100 countries. General Mills produces a wide range of products, including cereals, snacks, baking mixes, yogurt, ice cream, and frozen foods. The company also owns several popular brands, such as Cheerios, Nature Valley, Pillsbury, and Yoplait. General Mills is a publicly traded company, listed on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol GIS.
===History of General Mills===
General Mills was founded in 1928 by James Ford Bell, who had previously worked for the Washburn-Crosby Company. Bell wanted to create a company that could produce a variety of products, and he chose the name General Mills to reflect the company’s broad scope. The company’s first product was Gold Medal Flour, which was an instant success. Over the next few decades, General Mills continued to expand its [[Product Line|product line]], introducing new products such as Cheerios, Nature Valley, and Pillsbury. In the 1950s, the company began to diversify its business, acquiring companies in the foodservice, pet food, and restaurant industries.
