===Introduction to DuPont Analysis === 
DuPont Analysis is a financial analysis technique used to assess the profitability and performance of a company. It is a tool used to break down a company’s return on equity (ROE) into three components: asset turnover, financial leverage, and profitability. The DuPont Analysis is named after the DuPont Corporation, which developed the technique in the 1920s. The DuPont Analysis is a useful tool for investors, analysts, and managers to assess the performance of a company and identify areas for improvement.
