===Introduction to Dupont Analysis===

Dupont Analysis is a [[Financial Analysis|financial analysis]] technique used to evaluate a company’s [[Financial Performance|financial performance]]. It is a tool used to measure the [[Return On Equity|return on equity]] (ROE) of a company by breaking down the components of the ROE into three distinct parts: [[Profit Margin|profit margin]], [[Asset Turnover|asset turnover]], and financial leverage. Dupont Analysis is a useful tool for investors and analysts to gain insight into a company’s financial performance and to compare it to other companies in the same industry.
===Components of Dupont Analysis===

Dupont Analysis is composed of three components: profit margin, asset turnover, and financial leverage. Profit margin is a measure of how much profit a company makes relative to its total sales. Asset turnover is a measure of how efficiently a company is using its assets to generate sales. Financial leverage is a measure of how much debt a company has relative to its equity.
===Benefits of Dupont Analysis===

===Limitations of Dupont Analysis===
