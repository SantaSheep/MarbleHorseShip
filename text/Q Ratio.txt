===Introduction to Qratio===

Qratio is a financial term used to measure the relative value of a company’s equity to its debt. It is a measure of a company’s financial health and is used to assess the risk of investing in a company. Qratio is an important tool for investors and analysts to evaluate a company’s financial position and make informed decisions about investing in the company.
===Definition of Qratio===

Qratio is defined as the ratio of a company’s total equity to its total debt. It is calculated by dividing the total equity of a company by its total debt. The higher the Qratio, the better the financial health of the company. A Qratio of 1.0 or higher indicates that the company has more equity than debt and is in a strong financial position. A Qratio of less than 1.0 indicates that the company has more debt than equity and is in a weak financial position.
===Calculation of Qratio===

Qratio is calculated by dividing the total equity of a company by its total debt. Total equity is calculated by subtracting [[Total Liabilities|total liabilities]] from total assets. Total debt is calculated by adding long-term debt and short-term debt. The formula for calculating Qratio is:

Qratio = Total Equity / Total Debt
