===Introduction to American Express===

American Express is a global financial services company that provides a wide range of products and services to its customers. Founded in 1850, American Express is one of the oldest and most respected financial services companies in the world. The company is headquartered in New York City and operates in more than 130 countries around the world. American Express is a publicly traded company and is listed on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE).
===History of American Express===

American Express was founded in 1850 by Henry Wells, William Fargo, and John Warren Butterfield. The company was initially established as an express mail business, providing express delivery services to customers in the United States and Canada. In 1882, American Express began offering money orders and travelers’ checks, and in 1891, the company began offering its first charge card. In 1958, American Express introduced its first [[Credit Card|credit card]], and in 1966, the company launched its first travel rewards program.
===Business Model of American Express===

American Express operates a network-based business model. The company’s network consists of merchants, cardholders, and [[Financial Institution|financial institution]]s. American Express earns revenue by charging merchants a fee for each transaction made with an American Express card, and by charging cardholders an annual fee for their card. The company also earns revenue from interest on loans and investments, and from fees charged for services such as travel insurance and foreign currency exchange.
