===Introduction to Corporate Governance===
Corporate governance is the system of rules, practices, and processes by which a company is directed and managed. It involves balancing the interests of the many stakeholders involved in the business, such as shareholders, management, customers, suppliers, financiers, government, and the community. ===The Role of the Board of Directors===
The [[Board Of Directors|board of directors]] is the highest governing body of a company and is responsible for setting the company’s strategic direction, monitoring management performance, and ensuring that the company complies with applicable laws and regulations. The board of directors is typically composed of a mix of executive and non-executive directors, with the latter providing independent oversight of the company’s operations. The board of directors is responsible for appointing and overseeing the activities of the company’s senior management team, setting the company’s strategic direction, and ensuring that the company complies with applicable laws and regulations.
===Corporate Governance Principles===
Corporate governance principles are the set of rules and guidelines that a company follows in order to ensure that it is managed in an ethical and responsible manner. These principles are typically set out in the company’s corporate governance code, which is a document that outlines the company’s commitment to good corporate governance. The principles typically include the following: transparency, accountability, fairness, responsibility, and integrity.
===Corporate Governance Practices===
Corporate governance practices are the specific actions taken by a company to ensure that it is managed in an ethical and responsible manner. These practices typically include the following: establishing a board of directors, setting up a system of [[Internal Controls|internal controls]], implementing a code of conduct, and establishing a system of [[Risk Management|risk management]].
===Corporate Governance and Shareholder Value===
