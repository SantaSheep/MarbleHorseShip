The Bretton Woods Agreement was the result of a series of meetings held in the summer of 1944. The meetings were attended by representatives from 44 countries, including the United States, the United Kingdom, France, and the Soviet Union. The Bretton Woods Agreement established the rules for international monetary relations. It established the gold standard, which fixed the value of the U.S. dollar to gold at $35 per ounce.
