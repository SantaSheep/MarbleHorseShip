===Introduction to Return on Investment (ROI) === 
Return on Investment (ROI) is a measure of the profitability of an investment. It is calculated by dividing the net gain or loss of an investment by the total amount invested. ROI is used to measure the efficiency of an investment and to compare the performance of different investments. It is a key metric for investors and businesses alike, as it helps them to make informed decisions about where to allocate their resources.
===Calculating ROI === 
ROI is calculated by dividing the net gain or loss of an investment by the total amount invested. For example, if an investor invests $100 and earns a net gain of $20, the ROI would be 20%.
