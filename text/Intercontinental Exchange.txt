===Introduction to Intercontinental Exchange===

Intercontinental Exchange (ICE) is a leading global exchange operator and provider of data, technology, and market infrastructure. Founded in 2000, ICE operates exchanges, clearing houses, and data services for financial and commodity markets. ICE is headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia, and has offices in London, New York, Chicago, and Singapore. The company is listed on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and is a component of the S&P 500 index.
===History of Intercontinental Exchange===

Intercontinental Exchange was founded in 2000 by Jeffrey Sprecher, a former executive at Enron. Sprecher had previously founded the energy trading firm, EnronOnline, and was looking to create a new exchange that would provide a more efficient and transparent marketplace for energy trading. In 2002, ICE acquired the International Petroleum Exchange (IPE), which was the world’s first electronic energy exchange. This acquisition marked the beginning of ICE’s expansion into other [[Asset Classes|asset classes]], such as commodities, currencies, and derivatives.
