===Introduction to Cisco===

Cisco Systems, Inc. is a multinational technology conglomerate headquartered in San Jose, California. Founded in 1984, Cisco is one of the world’s largest networking equipment manufacturers and is a leader in the development of Internet Protocol (IP)-based networking technologies. Cisco’s products and services are used by businesses, governments, and consumers around the world to build and maintain their networks. Cisco is also a major provider of cloud-based services, such as its WebEx collaboration platform.
===History of Cisco===

Cisco was founded in 1984 by Leonard Bosack and Sandy Lerner, two Stanford University computer scientists. The company was initially focused on developing and selling routers, which are devices that connect computers and other devices on a network. In the early 1990s, Cisco began to expand its [[Product Line|product line]] to include switches, which are devices that control the flow of data on a network. By the late 1990s, Cisco had become the dominant provider of networking equipment and had established itself as a leader in the development of IP-based networking technologies.
===Cisco’s Business Model===

Cisco’s business model is based on the sale of networking equipment and services. The company’s products are sold to businesses, governments, and consumers around the world. Cisco also provides cloud-based services, such as its WebEx collaboration platform. In addition, Cisco provides technical support and consulting services to its customers.
===Cisco’s Financial Performance===

Cisco has been a highly profitable company since its founding. In 2019, the company reported total revenue of $51.9 billion, up from $48.7 billion in 2018. Cisco’s [[Net Income|net income]] for 2019 was $13.2 billion, up from $12.2 billion in 2018. The company’s stock price has also been steadily increasing over the past five years, reaching a high of $54.45 in 2019.
