===Introduction to Wells Fargo===

Wells Fargo is one of the largest financial services companies in the United States. It is a diversified financial services company that provides banking, insurance, investments, mortgage, and consumer and commercial finance services to individuals, businesses, and institutions. Wells Fargo is headquartered in San Francisco, California and has operations in more than 35 countries around the world. The company has more than 8,000 retail banking locations and more than 13,000 ATMs. Wells Fargo is one of the largest banks in the United States, with total assets of more than $1.9 trillion.
===History of Wells Fargo===

Wells Fargo was founded in 1852 by Henry Wells and William Fargo. The company was originally established as a stagecoach express service to transport gold and other goods between San Francisco and the East Coast. In 1866, Wells Fargo became the first American bank to offer international banking services. In the late 19th century, Wells Fargo expanded its operations to include banking, insurance, investments, and other financial services. In the early 20th century, Wells Fargo acquired several other banks and [[Financial Institution|financial institution]]s, including Wachovia and First Interstate Bank.
===Business Model of Wells Fargo===

Wells Fargo’s business model is based on providing a wide range of financial services to individuals, businesses, and institutions. The company’s core business is retail banking, which includes checking and savings accounts, [[Credit Card|credit card]]s, mortgages, and other consumer banking services. Wells Fargo also offers investment services, including [[Mutual Fund|mutual fund]]s, brokerage services, and retirement planning. The company also provides [[Commercial Bank|commercial bank]]ing services, including loans, lines of credit, and treasury management services. In addition, Wells Fargo offers insurance products, such as life, auto, and homeowners insurance.
===Financial Performance of Wells Fargo===

Wells Fargo has consistently reported strong [[Financial Performance|financial performance]]. Wells Fargo’s [[Return On Equity|return on equity]] was 11.3%, and its [[Return On Assets|return on assets]] was 1.2%. The company’s stock price has also performed well, with a total return of more than 20% in 2020.
===Social Impact of Wells Fargo===

Wells Fargo is committed to making a positive social impact in the communities it serves. The company has a long history of philanthropy and community involvement. Wells Fargo has donated more than $1 billion to charitable causes since 2009. The company also supports a variety of initiatives, such as [[Financial Literacy|financial literacy]] programs, small business development, and affordable housing.
