===Introduction to Kimberly Clark===

Kimberly-Clark Corporation is a global consumer products company that produces and markets a wide range of products for personal and health care, as well as for industrial and commercial use. The company is headquartered in Dallas, Texas and has operations in more than 35 countries. Kimberly-Clark is one of the world’s leading manufacturers of tissue, personal care, and health care products, as well as one of the largest producers of paper-based packaging products. The company’s products are sold in more than 175 countries and territories.
===History of Kimberly Clark===

Kimberly-Clark was founded in 1872 by John A. Kimberly, Havilah Babcock, Charles B. Clark, and Franklyn  Shattuck. The company was initially established as a paper mill in Neenah, Wisconsin. In the early 1900s, the company began to expand its [[Product Line|product line]] to include consumer products such as facial tissue, paper towels, and diapers. In the 1950s, the company began to expand its operations internationally, and by the 1970s, Kimberly-Clark had become a global leader in the consumer products industry.
