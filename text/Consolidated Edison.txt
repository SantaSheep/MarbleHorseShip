===Introduction to Consolidated Edison===

Consolidated Edison, Inc. (Con Edison) is a publicly traded energy company headquartered in New York City. It is the largest energy company in the United States and one of the largest in the world. Con Edison provides electricity, natural gas, and steam to customers in New York City and Westchester County. It also provides energy-related services to customers in other parts of the United States. Con Edison is a subsidiary of Consolidated Edison Company of New York, Inc. (CECONY), which is the [[Parent Company|parent company]] of Con Edison and its subsidiaries.
===History of Consolidated Edison===

Con Edison was founded in 1823 as the New York Gas Light Company. It was the first gas utility in the United States and the first to provide gas lighting to the streets of New York City. In 1884, the company changed its name to Consolidated Gas Company of New York. In 1936, the company changed its name again to Consolidated Edison Company of New York, Inc. (CECONY). In 1997, CECONY spun off its electric and gas businesses into two separate companies: Con Edison and Orange and Rockland Utilities, Inc. (O&R).
