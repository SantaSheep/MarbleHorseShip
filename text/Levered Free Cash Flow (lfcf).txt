===Introduction to Levered Free Cash Flow (LFCF) === 
Levered free cash flow (LFCF) is a financial metric used to measure the amount of cash available to a company after all of its debt obligations have been met. It is a measure of a company’s financial health and is used to assess the company’s ability to pay its debts and generate profits. LFCF is an important metric for investors and analysts to consider when evaluating a company’s financial performance.
===What is Levered Free Cash Flow (LFCF)? === 
Levered free cash flow (LFCF) is a measure of a company’s financial health that takes into account the company’s debt obligations. It is calculated by subtracting the company’s total debt payments from its operating cash flow. LFCF is a measure of the cash available to a company after all of its debt obligations have been met. It is an important metric for investors and analysts to consider when evaluating a company’s financial performance.
