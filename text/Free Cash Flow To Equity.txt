===Introduction to Free Cash Flow to Equity===

Free [[Cash Flow|Cash Flow]] to Equity (FCFE) is a financial metric used to measure the amount of cash available to the equity holders of a company after all expenses, investments, and debt payments have been made. It is a measure of the company’s ability to generate cash for its shareholders and is an important indicator of the company’s financial health. FCFE is used by investors to assess the company’s ability to generate cash for its shareholders and is an important indicator of the company’s financial health.
===Definition of Free Cash Flow to Equity===

[[Free Cash Flow|Free Cash Flow]] to Equity (FCFE) is defined as the amount of cash available to the equity holders of a company after all expenses, investments, and debt payments have been made. It is calculated by subtracting the company’s [[Capital Expenditure|capital expenditure]]s and debt payments from its [[Operating Cash Flow|operating cash flow]]. FCFE is a measure of the company’s ability to generate cash for its shareholders and is an important indicator of the company’s financial health.
===Calculation of Free Cash Flow to Equity===

Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE) is calculated by subtracting the company’s capital expenditures and debt payments from its operating cash flow. The formula for calculating FCFE is as follows:

FCFE = Operating Cash Flow – Capital Expenditures – Debt Payments
