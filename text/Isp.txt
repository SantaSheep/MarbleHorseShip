===Introduction to ISP===

An Internet Service Provider (ISP) is a company that provides access to the Internet. ISPs are responsible for providing the infrastructure and services necessary for users to connect to the Internet. ISPs are typically divided into two categories: residential and business. Residential ISPs provide services to individual households, while business ISPs provide services to businesses and organizations. ISPs are typically regulated by the government and must adhere to certain standards and regulations.
===Definition of ISP===

===History of ISP===

The first ISPs were established in the early 1990s, when the Internet was still in its infancy. At the time, ISPs were typically small, local companies that provided dial-up access to the Internet. As the Internet grew in popularity, ISPs began to expand their services to include broadband access, such as cable and DSL. Today, ISPs are much larger and provide a variety of services, such as web hosting, email, and VoIP.
