===Introduction to Short Squeeze === 
A short squeeze is a phenomenon that occurs when a heavily shorted stock or commodity suddenly experiences a sharp increase in price. This increase in price is usually caused by a lack of supply and an increase in demand, which can be triggered by a variety of factors. Short squeezes can be extremely profitable for investors who are able to capitalize on the situation, but they can also be extremely risky. In this article, we will discuss what a short squeeze is, how it works, examples of short squeezes, and the risks associated with them.
===What is a Short Squeeze? === 
A short squeeze is a situation in which a heavily shorted stock or commodity suddenly experiences a sharp increase in price. This increase in price is usually caused by a lack of supply and an increase in demand, which can be triggered by a variety of factors. Short squeezes can be extremely profitable for investors who are able to capitalize on the situation, but they can also be extremely risky.
===How Does a Short Squeeze Work? === 
A short squeeze occurs when a heavily shorted stock or commodity suddenly experiences a sharp increase in price. This increase in price is usually caused by a lack of supply and an increase in demand, which can be triggered by a variety of factors. When the price of the stock or commodity increases, the short sellers are forced to buy back the shares they have sold in order to cover their positions. This buying pressure can cause the price of the stock or commodity to increase even further, resulting in a short squeeze.
===Examples of Short Squeezes === 
One of the most famous examples of a short squeeze occurred in 2008 when the price of oil suddenly spiked. This spike was caused by a combination of factors, including speculation, geopolitical tensions, and a lack of supply. Another example of a short squeeze occurred in 2013 when the stock of the video game company GameStop suddenly surged. This surge was caused by a combination of factors, including speculation, a lack of supply, and a surge in demand from retail investors.
