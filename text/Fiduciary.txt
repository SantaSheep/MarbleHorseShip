===Introduction to Fiduciary===

Fiduciary is a term used to describe a person or entity that is legally obligated to act in the best interests of another person or entity. This legal obligation is known as a fiduciary duty and is typically found in relationships between a trustee and a beneficiary, a lawyer and a client, or a financial advisor and a client. Fiduciary duties are based on trust and require the fiduciary to act in the best interests of the beneficiary or client, even if it means sacrificing their own interests.
===Definition of Fiduciary===

A fiduciary is a person or entity that has a legal obligation to act in the best interests of another person or entity. This legal obligation is known as a fiduciary duty and is typically found in relationships between a trustee and a beneficiary, a lawyer and a client, or a financial advisor and a client. The fiduciary is expected to act in the best interests of the beneficiary or client, even if it means sacrificing their own interests.
===Types of Fiduciary===

There are several types of fiduciaries, including trustees, executors, guardians, attorneys, financial advisors, and corporate officers. A trustee is a person or entity that holds legal title to property for the benefit of another person or entity. An executor is a person or entity appointed by a court to manage the estate of a deceased person. A guardian is a person or entity appointed by a court to manage the affairs of a minor or incapacitated person. An attorney is a person or entity that provides legal advice and representation to a client. A financial advisor is a person or entity that provides financial advice and services to a client. A corporate officer is a person or entity that is responsible for the management of a corporation.
===Duties of a Fiduciary===

The duties of a fiduciary are based on trust and require the fiduciary to act in the best interests of the beneficiary or client. This means that the fiduciary must act with the utmost good faith, loyalty, and care when making decisions on behalf of the beneficiary or client. The fiduciary must also avoid conflicts of interest and must not use their position for personal gain.
