===Introduction to Centene Corporation===

Centene Corporation is a Fortune 500 company that provides managed care services to government-sponsored healthcare programs, including Medicaid, Medicare, and the Children’s Health Insurance Program (CHIP). The company is headquartered in St. Louis, Missouri and operates in all 50 states, as well as the District of Columbia and Puerto Rico. Centene is one of the largest managed care organizations in the United States, with over 11 million members and a network of over 1.2 million providers. The company also provides specialty services, such as behavioral health, pharmacy benefits management, and long-term care services.
===History of Centene Corporation===

Centene Corporation was founded in 1984 by Michael F. Neidorff as a provider of Medicaid managed care services. The company initially operated in Missouri and expanded to other states over the next decade. In 1996, Centene became a publicly traded company and began to expand its services to include Medicare and CHIP. In the 2000s, the company continued to expand its services and its geographic reach, and in 2010, Centene acquired Health Net, Inc., a California-based managed care organization.
===Business Model of Centene Corporation===

Centene’s business model is based on providing managed care services to government-sponsored healthcare programs. The company contracts with state and federal governments to provide healthcare services to Medicaid, Medicare, and CHIP beneficiaries. Centene also contracts with providers to provide services to its members. The company’s services include primary care, specialty care, behavioral health, pharmacy benefits management, and long-term care services.
