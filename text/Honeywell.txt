===Introduction to Honeywell===

Honeywell is a global technology and manufacturing company that provides products and services to customers in a variety of industries. Founded in 1906, Honeywell has grown to become a leader in the aerospace, building technologies, and performance materials and technologies industries. The company has operations in more than 100 countries and employs over 130,000 people worldwide. Honeywell’s products and services are used in a variety of industries, including aerospace, automotive, chemicals, construction, defense, energy, healthcare, industrial, and transportation.
===History of Honeywell===

Honeywell was founded in 1906 by Mark  Honeywell as the Honeywell Heating Specialty Company. The company initially focused on the development and production of heating systems and controls. In 1927, Honeywell merged with Minneapolis Heat Regulator Company and became the Minneapolis-Honeywell Regulator Company. The company continued to expand its product offerings and in 1936, Honeywell introduced the first automatic pilot for aircraft.

In the 1950s, Honeywell began to diversify its product offerings and began to focus on the development of electronic and computer-based products. In the 1960s, Honeywell acquired several companies, including Sperry Rand Corporation, which allowed the company to expand its product offerings to include computers and other electronic products. In the 1970s, Honeywell began to focus on the development of aerospace and defense products.
