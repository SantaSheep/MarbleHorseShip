===Introduction to Johnson Controls===

Johnson Controls is a global diversified technology and multi-industrial leader serving a wide range of customers in more than 150 countries. The company’s 130,000 employees create intelligent buildings, efficient energy solutions, integrated infrastructure and next-generation transportation systems that work seamlessly together to deliver on the promise of smart cities and communities. Johnson Controls’ commitment to sustainability dates back to its roots in 1885, with the invention of the first electric room thermostat. Today, the company is committed to helping customers advance their sustainability goals through its products and services.
===History of Johnson Controls===

Johnson Controls was founded in 1885 by Warren S. Johnson, an inventor and entrepreneur. Johnson developed the first electric room thermostat, which revolutionized the way people heated and cooled their homes. Since then, Johnson Controls has grown to become a global leader in the building and energy management industry. The company has expanded its product portfolio to include a wide range of solutions for commercial, industrial, and residential applications. Johnson Controls has also developed innovative technologies to help customers reduce energy consumption and improve operational efficiency.
===Business Model of Johnson Controls===

Johnson Controls’ business model is focused on providing integrated solutions to customers in the building and energy management industry. The company’s products and services are designed to help customers reduce energy consumption, improve operational efficiency, and create intelligent buildings. Johnson Controls’ solutions are tailored to meet the needs of each customer, and the company works closely with customers to ensure that their solutions are tailored to their specific needs. Johnson Controls also provides a range of services, such as installation, maintenance, and repair, to ensure that customers get the most out of their solutions.
