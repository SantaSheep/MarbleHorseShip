===Introduction to Interest Coverage Ratio === 
Interest coverage ratio is a financial metric used to measure a company’s ability to pay its interest expenses on its outstanding debt. It is an important indicator of a company’s financial health and is used by lenders and investors to assess the risk of lending money to a company.
===What is Interest Coverage Ratio? === 
Interest coverage ratio is a financial metric that measures a company’s ability to pay its interest expenses on its outstanding debt. It is calculated by dividing a company’s earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT) by its interest expenses. A higher interest coverage ratio indicates that a company is more likely to be able to pay its interest expenses and is therefore considered to be a safer investment.
