===Introduction to Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) === 
Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) are a set of rules and regulations that govern the preparation of financial statements. These principles are used to ensure that financial statements are prepared in a consistent and reliable manner. GAAP is the most widely accepted accounting framework in the United States and is used by most public companies.
===Overview of GAAP Requirements === 
GAAP requires that financial statements be prepared in accordance with certain principles and standards. These principles and standards include the recognition, measurement, and disclosure of financial information. The primary objective of GAAP is to provide a consistent and reliable basis for financial reporting.
===Financial Statement Preparation Under GAAP === 
Financial statements prepared under GAAP must include the following elements: assets, liabilities, equity, income, expenses, and cash flows. The financial statements must also include a statement of changes in equity, a statement of cash flows, and a statement of comprehensive income.
===GAAP and Internal Controls === 
GAAP requires that companies have internal controls in place to ensure that financial statements are prepared in accordance with GAAP. Internal controls are designed to ensure that financial information is accurate and reliable. Internal controls also help to prevent fraud and misappropriation of funds.
