===Introduction to NPV===

Net [[Present Value|Present Value]] (NPV) is a financial concept used to evaluate the profitability of a project or investment. It is a measure of the present value of future [[Cash Flow|cash flow]]s, taking into account the [[Time Value Of Money|time value of money]]. NPV is used to compare the profitability of different investments and to determine whether a project should be undertaken.
===Definition of NPV===

Net Present Value (NPV) is the difference between the present value of the expected future cash flows of an investment and the initial cost of the investment. It is a measure of the profitability of an investment, taking into account the time value of money. NPV is calculated by discounting the expected future cash flows of an investment at a given [[Rate Of Return|rate of return]] and subtracting the initial cost of the investment.

===Advantages of NPV===

NPV is a useful tool for evaluating the profitability of an investment. It takes into account the time value of money, which is important when comparing investments with different time horizons. NPV also allows for the comparison of investments with different cash flows, as it takes into account the present value of future cash flows.
===Disadvantages of NPV===

The main disadvantage of NPV is that it is based on estimates of future cash flows, which may be inaccurate. Additionally, NPV does not take into account the risk associated with an investment, which can be an important factor in determining whether an investment should be undertaken.
