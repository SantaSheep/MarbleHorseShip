===Introduction to Comps===

Comps, short for Comparables, is a financial term used to refer to the comparison of similar assets or companies in order to determine the value of a particular asset or company. Comps are used by investors, analysts, and other financial professionals to evaluate the potential of an investment or to compare the performance of different companies. Comps are used to compare the [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] of companies in the same industry, as well as to compare the performance of companies in different industries. Comps can also be used to compare the performance of different assets, such as stocks, bonds, and [[Real Estate|real estate]].
===Types of Comps===

There are several different types of Comps that can be used to compare the performance of different companies or assets. The most common type of Comps is the Price-to-Earnings (P/E) ratio, which is used to compare the price of a company’s stock to its earnings.
