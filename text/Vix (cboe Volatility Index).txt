===Introduction to VIX (CBOE Volatility Index) === 
The CBOE Volatility Index, or VIX, is a measure of market volatility that is calculated and published by the Chicago Board Options Exchange (CBOE). It is often referred to as the “fear index” or the “fear gauge” because it is used to gauge investor sentiment and market risk. VIX is a popular tool for investors to use to assess the level of risk in the markets and to make decisions about their investments.
===What is VIX? === 
VIX is a measure of the expected volatility of the S&P 500 index over the next 30 days. It is calculated using a weighted average of the implied volatilities of a wide range of S&P 500 options. The VIX is expressed as a percentage and is often used as a gauge of investor sentiment and market risk.
===How is VIX Calculated? === 
The VIX is calculated using a weighted average of the implied volatilities of a wide range of S&P 500 options. The VIX is calculated using a formula that takes into account the prices of both calls and puts on the S&P 500 index. The formula takes into account the strike prices, expiration dates, and implied volatilities of the options.
