===Introduction to United Parcel Service===
United Parcel Service (UPS) is a global package delivery and [[Supply Chain|supply chain]] management company. Founded in 1907, UPS has grown to become one of the world’s largest package delivery companies, with operations in more than 220 countries and territories. UPS is headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia, and employs more than 500,000 people worldwide. The company’s core business is the delivery of packages and documents, but it also provides a range of other services, including freight forwarding, customs brokerage, and supply chain management.
===History of United Parcel Service===
UPS was founded in 1907 by James E. Casey and Claude Ryan in Seattle, Washington. The company began as a messenger service, delivering packages and documents for local businesses. In 1913, the company began offering parcel delivery services, and in 1919, it began offering international delivery services. In the 1930s, UPS began offering air delivery services, and in the 1950s, it began offering ground delivery services. In the 1970s, UPS began offering freight forwarding services, and in the 1980s, it began offering customs brokerage services. In the 1990s, UPS began offering supply chain management services.
