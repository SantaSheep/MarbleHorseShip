===Introduction to United Airlines Holdings===
United Airlines Holdings, Inc. (UAL) is a major American airline [[Holding Company|holding company]] headquartered in Chicago, Illinois. It is the [[Parent Company|parent company]] of United Airlines, Inc., the world’s third-largest airline in terms of revenue and the largest airline in the United States. UAL is a publicly traded company listed on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and is a component of the S&P 500 index. The company was formed in 2010 following the merger of United Airlines and Continental Airlines. UAL is a member of the Star Alliance, the world’s largest airline alliance.
===History of United Airlines Holdings===
United Airlines Holdings traces its roots back to 1926 when William Boeing founded the Boeing Air Transport Company. In 1931, Boeing merged with Pratt & Whitney to form United Aircraft and Transport Corporation. In 1934, the company was reorganized and renamed United Airlines. In 2010, United Airlines merged with Continental Airlines to form United Airlines Holdings, Inc. The merger created the world’s largest airline in terms of revenue and the largest airline in the United States.

United Airlines Holdings has seen strong [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] in recent years. In 2019, the company reported total revenue of $41.8 billion, up from $37.9 billion in 2018. [[Operating Income|Operating income]] for 2019 was $3.3 billion, up from $2.7 billion in 2018. [[Net Income|Net income]] for 2019 was $2.2 billion, up from $1.7 billion in 2018. The company’s total assets as of December 31, 2019 were $45.2 billion, up from $41.3 billion in 2018.
===Management of United Airlines Holdings===
United Airlines Holdings is led by CEO Oscar Munoz, who has been with the company since 2015. The company’s [[Board Of Directors|board of directors]] is chaired by Robert Milton, who has been with the company since 2010. The board also includes several other prominent business leaders, including former U.S. Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice and former U.S. Secretary of Defense Robert Gates.
