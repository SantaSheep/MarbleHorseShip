===Introduction to Visa Inc.===

Visa Inc. is a [[Global Payments|global payments]] technology company that connects consumers, businesses, [[Financial Institution|financial institution]]s, and governments in more than 200 countries and territories. Visa Inc. operates the world’s largest retail electronic payments network and is one of the most recognized global financial services brands. Visa Inc. is headquartered in Foster City, California and has more than 20,000 employees worldwide.
===History of Visa Inc.===

Visa Inc. was founded in 1958 as BankAmericard, a [[Joint Venture|joint venture]] between [[Bank Of America|Bank of America]] and several other California banks. In 1976, BankAmericard changed its name to Visa and began to expand its operations internationally. In 2007, Visa Inc. became a publicly traded company and is now listed on the New York Stock Exchange.
===Business Model of Visa Inc.===

Visa Inc.’s business model is based on providing payment solutions to consumers, businesses, financial institutions, and governments. The company’s core products and services include credit, debit, prepaid, and commercial payment cards, as well as payment processing services. Visa Inc. also offers a range of value-added services such as fraud protection, data security, and loyalty programs.
===Financial Performance of Visa Inc.===

Visa Inc. has experienced strong [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] in recent years. In the [[[[Fiscal Year|Fiscal Year]] End|fiscal year end]]ing September 30, 2020, the company reported total revenue of $22.9 billion, an increase of 11% from the prior year. [[Net Income|Net income]] for the same period was $7.7 billion, an increase of 14% from the prior year.
===Recent Developments of Visa Inc.===

Visa Inc. has made several strategic acquisitions in recent years to expand its product and service offerings. In 2020, the company acquired Plaid, a financial technology company that provides APIs for banks and fintechs to securely connect to consumer financial accounts. In 2021, Visa Inc. also acquired Earthport, a cross-border payments network that provides access to more than 60 countries.
