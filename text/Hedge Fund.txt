===Introduction to Hedgefunds===
A hedge fund is an investment fund that pools capital from [[Accredited Investor|accredited investor]]s or institutional investors and invests in a variety of assets, often with complex portfolio-construction and [[Risk Management|risk management]] techniques. It is administered by a professional investment management firm, and often structured as a [[Limited Partnership|limited partnership]], limited liability company, or similar vehicle. Hedge funds are generally distinct from [[Mutual Fund|mutual fund]]s, as their use of leverage is not capped by regulators, and distinct from [[Private Equity|private equity]] funds, as the majority of hedge funds invest in relatively [[Liquid Asset|liquid asset]]s.
===Types of Hedgefunds===
Hedge funds can be classified into different types based on their investment strategies. The most common types of hedge funds are long/short equity, event-driven, macro, relative value, and multi-strategy. Long/short equity hedge funds invest in both long and short positions in stocks, with the goal of generating positive returns regardless of the direction of the [[Stock Market|stock market]]. Event-driven hedge funds focus on corporate events such as mergers, acquisitions, and bankruptcies. Macro hedge funds invest in global macroeconomic trends, such as currency, [[Interest Rate|interest rate]], and commodity price movements. Relative value hedge funds invest in securities that are mispriced relative to each other, such as convertible bonds and fixed income securities. Multi-strategy hedge funds invest in a variety of strategies, such as long/short equity, event-driven, macro, and relative value.

===Benefits of Hedgefunds===
Hedge funds offer a number of benefits to investors. Hedge funds are able to employ a variety of strategies to generate returns, which can provide investors with diversification and the potential for higher returns. Hedge funds also have the ability to use leverage, which can increase returns. Additionally, hedge funds are able to invest in a variety of assets, including stocks, bonds, commodities, and currencies. This allows investors to access a wide range of markets and [[Asset Classes|asset classes]].
