===Introduction to Marginal Utility===

Marginal utility is an economic concept that describes the additional satisfaction or benefit that a consumer receives from consuming one additional unit of a good or service. It is a measure of the change in total utility that a consumer experiences when consuming one additional unit of a good or service. The concept of marginal utility is used to explain consumer behavior and to determine the optimal level of consumption of a good or service.
===Theoretical Framework of Marginal Utility===

The concept of marginal utility is based on the law of diminishing marginal utility. This law states that as a consumer consumes more of a good or service, the additional satisfaction or benefit that they receive from consuming one additional unit of the good or service will decrease. This is because the consumer has already consumed a certain amount of the good or service and the additional satisfaction or benefit that they receive from consuming one additional unit of the good or service will be less than the satisfaction or benefit that they received from consuming the first unit.

This is because as the price of a good or service increases, the marginal utility of the good or service decreases, and the consumer will be less willing to purchase the good or service.

Marginal utility is also used to explain the [[Law Of Diminishing Marginal Return|law of diminishing marginal return]]s.
