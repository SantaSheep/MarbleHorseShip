===Introduction to Free Cash Flow (FCF) === 
Free Cash Flow (FCF) is a measure of a company’s financial performance that is used to assess the amount of cash available to the company after all expenses have been paid. It is a key metric used by investors and analysts to evaluate a company’s financial health and performance. FCF is calculated by subtracting capital expenditures from operating cash flow. It is a measure of the cash that a company has available to pay dividends, buy back stock, pay down debt, or reinvest in the business.
===Understanding FCF Calculation === 
FCF is calculated by subtracting capital expenditures from operating cash flow. Operating cash flow is the amount of cash generated from a company’s operations, such as sales, expenses, and taxes. Capital expenditures are the funds used to purchase long-term assets, such as property, plant, and equipment. FCF is a measure of the cash that a company has available to pay dividends, buy back stock, pay down debt, or reinvest in the business.
===Benefits of FCF === 
FCF is a valuable metric for investors and analysts because it provides insight into a company’s financial health and performance. It is a measure of the cash that a company has available to pay dividends, buy back stock, pay down debt, or reinvest in the business. By understanding FCF, investors and analysts can make more informed decisions about a company’s financial health and performance.
===FCF and Equity Valuation === 
FCF is also a key metric used to value a company’s equity. Equity valuation is the process of determining the value of a company’s stock. By understanding FCF, investors and analysts can make more informed decisions about a company’s equity value.
===FCF and Debt Valuation === 
FCF is also a key metric used to value a company’s debt. Debt valuation is the process of determining the value of a company’s debt. By understanding FCF, investors and analysts can make more informed decisions about a company’s debt value.
