===Introduction to Etsy===
Etsy is an online marketplace for handmade and vintage goods. It was founded in 2005 by Rob Kalin, Chris Maguire, and Haim Schoppik. Etsy is headquartered in Brooklyn, New York and has offices in Berlin, Dublin, London, Paris, and Tokyo. Etsy is a publicly traded company on the Nasdaq stock exchange and has a [[Market Capitalization|market capitalization]] of over $7 billion. Etsy has over 60 million active buyers and sellers and over 2.5 million active shops. Etsy is a global platform for creative entrepreneurs to sell their handmade and vintage goods.
===History of Etsy===
Etsy was founded in 2005 by Rob Kalin, Chris Maguire, and Haim Schoppik. The company was initially funded by a $3 million investment from Union Square Ventures. Etsy was launched in June 2005 and quickly gained traction as a platform for creative entrepreneurs to sell their handmade and vintage goods. In 2007, Etsy raised an additional $27 million in [[Venture Capital|venture capital]] funding. In 2011, Etsy went public and began trading on the Nasdaq stock exchange.
===Business Model of Etsy===
Etsy’s business model is based on a marketplace model. Etsy charges a listing fee of $0.20 per item listed and a transaction fee of 5% of the total sale price. Etsy also offers additional services such as shipping labels, payment processing, and advertising. Etsy also offers a subscription service called Etsy Plus which provides additional features and benefits to sellers.
===Financial Performance of Etsy===
Etsy has seen strong [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] over the past few years. In 2020, Etsy reported revenue of $603 million, up from $441 million in 2019. Etsy’s gross merchandise sales (GMS) also increased from $3.93 billion in 2019 to $5.84 billion in 2020. Etsy’s [[Net Income|net income]] also increased from $76 million in 2019 to $122 million in 2020.
