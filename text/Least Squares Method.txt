===Introduction to Least Squares Method===

The [[Least Squares|Least Squares]] Method (LSM) is a mathematical technique used to estimate the parameters of a linear regression model. It is a widely used method in statistics and economics for fitting a linear model to a set of data points. The method is based on the principle of minimizing the sum of the squared errors between the observed data points and the model. The LSM is used to estimate the parameters of a linear regression model, which is a model that describes the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables.
===History of Least Squares Method===

The Least Squares Method was first developed by the French mathematician Adrien-Marie Legendre in the early 19th century. Legendre was attempting to find the best fit line for a set of data points. He developed the method of minimizing the sum of the squared errors between the observed data points and the model. This method is now known as the Least Squares Method.
===Applications of Least Squares Method===

The Least Squares Method is used in a variety of fields, including economics, engineering, and statistics. In economics, the method is used to estimate the parameters of a linear regression model, which is a model that describes the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables. In engineering, the method is used to estimate the parameters of a system of equations. In statistics, the method is used to estimate the parameters of a [[Probability Distribution|probability distribution]].
===Advantages and Disadvantages of Least Squares Method===

The Least Squares Method has several advantages. It is a simple and efficient method for estimating the parameters of a linear regression model. It is also relatively easy to implement and can be used with a variety of data sets. Additionally, the method is robust and can be used with data sets that contain outliers.

However, the Least Squares Method also has some disadvantages. It assumes that the data points are normally distributed, which may not always be the case. Additionally, the method is sensitive to outliers, which can lead to inaccurate estimates. Finally, the method does no[[T Account|t account]] for non-linear relationships between the dependent and independent variables.
