===Introduction to Comcast===

Comcast Corporation is an American telecommunications conglomerate that is the largest broadcasting and cable television company in the world by revenue. It is the second-largest pay-TV company after AT&T, the largest cable TV company and largest home Internet service provider in the United States, and the nation's third-largest home telephone service provider. Comcast services U.S. residential and commercial customers in 40 states and in the District of Columbia. The company's headquarters are located in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.
===History of Comcast===

Comcast was founded in 1963 by Ralph J. Roberts, Daniel Aaron, and Julian A. Brodsky. The company began as a small cable television operator in Tupelo, Mississippi. In 1969, the company was renamed Comcast Corporation and went public on the NASDAQ stock exchange. In the 1980s, Comcast began to expand its operations, acquiring several cable systems in the United States and Canada. In 2002, Comcast acquired AT&T Broadband, which made it the largest cable television provider in the United States.
===Comcast’s Business Model===

Comcast’s business model is based on providing cable television, high-speed internet, telephone, and other services to residential and commercial customers. The company also owns and operates several cable networks, including NBCUniversal, which it acquired in 2011. Comcast also owns and operates several regional sports networks, as well as the Xfinity streaming service.

Comcast has had a significant impact on the economy. The company is one of the largest employers in the United States, with over 200,000 employees.
