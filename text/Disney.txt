===Introduction to Disney===

The Walt Disney Company, commonly known as Disney, is an American diversified multinational mass media and entertainment conglomerate. It is one of the world’s largest media conglomerates, with assets encompassing film, television, theme parks, music, publishing, and more. Disney was founded in 1923 by brothers Walt and Roy Disney and is headquartered in Burbank, California. The company is best known for its iconic characters, such as Mickey Mouse, and its many films, such as Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs, The Lion King, and Frozen.
===History of Disney===

Disney was founded in 1923 by brothers Walt and Roy Disney. The company began as a small animation studio, producing short films and cartoons. In 1928, the company released its first feature-length animated film, Steamboat Willie, which featured the iconic character Mickey Mouse. This film was a major success and helped to establish Disney as a leader in the animation industry.

Throughout the 1930s and 1940s, Disney continued to produce animated films, including Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs, Pinocchio, and Fantasia. In 1955, the company opened its first theme park, Disneyland, in Anaheim, California. This park was an immediate success and helped to further establish Disney as a leader in the entertainment industry.
===Disney’s Business Model===

Disney’s business model is based on creating and distributing content across multiple platforms. The company produces films, television shows, and other content for its own networks, such as the Disney Channel, ABC, and ESPN. Disney also produces content for third-party networks, such as Netflix and Hulu. Additionally, Disney owns and operates theme parks, resorts, and cruise lines.
===Disney’s Financial Performance===

Disney’s [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] has been strong in recent years. In 2019, the company reported total revenue of $69.6 billion, up from $59.4 billion in 2018. The company’s [[Net Income|net income]] also increased from $12.6 billion in 2018 to $14.3 billion in 2019. Disney’s strong financial performance is largely due to its successful content production and distribution business.
===Disney’s Impact on the Economy===

Disney has had a significant impact on the global economy. The company’s theme parks, resorts, and cruise lines have created thousands of jobs and generated billions of dollars in revenue. Additionally, Disney’s content production and distribution business has helped to create new markets and expand existing ones.
