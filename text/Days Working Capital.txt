===Introduction to Days Working Capital === 
Days Working Capital (DWC) is a financial metric used to measure a companyâ€™s liquidity and ability to pay its short-term obligations. It is calculated by dividing the total current assets of a company by its total current liabilities. The result is expressed in days, which indicates how many days a company can cover its current liabilities with its current assets. This metric is important for investors and creditors to assess the financial health of a company.
