===Introduction to Bullish Engulfing Pattern===

The Bullish Engulfing Pattern is a [[Technical Analysis|technical analysis]] chart pattern that is used to identify potential reversals in the direction of a security’s price. It is a two-candle pattern that consists of a small bearish candle followed by a larger bullish candle. The bullish candle completely “engulfs” the bearish candle, hence the name. This pattern is used by traders to identify potential buying opportunities in a security.
===Anatomy of a Bullish Engulfing Pattern===

The Bullish Engulfing Pattern consists of two candles. The first candle is a bearish candle, which is characterized by a lower opening and closing price. This candle is followed by a larger bullish candle, which is characterized by a higher opening and closing price. The bullish candle completely “engulfs” the bearish candle, hence the name.
===How to Identify a Bullish Engulfing Pattern===

The Bullish Engulfing Pattern is relatively easy to identify. The pattern consists of two candles, a bearish candle followed by a larger bullish candle. The bullish candle must completely “engulf” the bearish candle, meaning that the opening and closing prices of the bullish candle must be higher than the opening and closing prices of the bearish candle.
