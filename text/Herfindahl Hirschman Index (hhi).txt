===Introduction to Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) === 
The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) is a measure of market concentration used by economists and regulators to assess the level of competition in a given market. It is a widely used tool for evaluating the competitive environment of an industry and is used to determine the level of competition in a market. The HHI is calculated by summing the squares of the market shares of all firms in the market.
===Definition of HHI ===
