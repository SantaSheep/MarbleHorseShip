===Introduction to Cash Ratio === 
Cash ratio is a financial metric used to measure a company’s liquidity and ability to pay its short-term obligations. It is calculated by dividing a company’s total cash and cash equivalents by its current liabilities. The cash ratio is a more conservative measure of liquidity than the current ratio, as it only considers the most liquid assets.
