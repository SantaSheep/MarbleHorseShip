===Introduction to Inverted Yield Curve===

An inverted [[Yield Curve|yield curve]] is a type of yield curve in which long-term [[Debt Instrument|debt instrument]]s have a lower yield than short-term debt instruments of the same credit quality. This phenomenon is the opposite of the normal yield curve, which typically has long-term debt instruments yielding more than short-term debt instruments. An inverted yield curve is often seen as a sign of an impending recession.
