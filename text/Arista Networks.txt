===Introduction to Arista Networks===

Arista Networks is a computer networking company that designs, manufactures, and sells high-performance, low-latency, cloud-networking solutions. Founded in 2004, Arista Networks has become a leader in the industry, providing customers with reliable, secure, and high-performance networking solutions. Arista Networks has a wide range of products and services, including switches, routers, and software-defined networking (SDN) solutions. Arista Networks is headquartered in Santa Clara, California, and has offices in the United States, Europe, and Asia.
===History of Arista Networks===

Arista Networks was founded in 2004 by Andy Bechtolsheim, David Cheriton, and Kenneth Duda. Bechtolsheim and Cheriton were both professors at Stanford University, and Duda was a former employee of Cisco Systems. The company was initially funded by [[Venture Capital|venture capital]] firms, including Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers and Sequoia Capital.

In 2006, Arista Networks released its first product, the Arista 7500E switch. This switch was designed to provide high-performance, low-latency networking solutions for data centers. Since then, Arista Networks has released a wide range of products and services, including switches, routers, and software-defined networking (SDN) solutions.
