===Introduction to Quantitative Easing===
Quantitative easing (QE) is a [[Monetary Policy|monetary policy]] tool used by central banks to stimulate [[Economic Growth|economic growth]]. It involves the purchase of government bonds and other [[Financial Asset|financial asset]]s from banks and other [[Financial Institution|financial institution]]s in order to increase the [[Money Supply|money supply]] and lower [[Interest Rate|interest rate]]s. This policy is used when traditional monetary policy tools, such as lowering the interest rate, are not effective.

===Types of Quantitative Easing===
There are two main types of quantitative easing: conventional and unconventional. Conventional quantitative easing involves the purchase of government bonds and other financial assets from banks and other financial institutions. Unconventional quantitative easing involves the purchase of assets from non-financial institutions, such as mortgage-backed securities and corporate bonds.
===Benefits of Quantitative Easing===
Quantitative easing can have a number of benefits for an economy. It can help to stimulate economic growth by increasing the money supply and lowering interest rates. This can lead to increased investment and consumption, which can help to create jobs and boost economic growth. Additionally, quantitative easing can help to reduce the risk of deflation, which can lead to a decrease in economic activity.
===Risks of Quantitative Easing===
Quantitative easing can also have some risks. It can lead to an increase in inflation, which can reduce the [[Purchasing Power|purchasing power]] of consumers. Additionally, it can lead to an increase in asset prices, which can lead to asset bubbles. Finally, it can lead to an increase in government debt, which can lead to an increase in government borrowing costs.
