===Introduction to Qualified Institutional Placement (QIP) === 
Qualified Institutional Placement (QIP) is a process of raising capital by listed companies in India. It is a process of issuing equity shares, fully and partly convertible debentures, or any securities other than warrants that are convertible to equity shares to qualified institutional buyers (QIBs). QIPs are a cost-effective way for companies to raise capital without going through the lengthy process of a public issue.
===Benefits of QIP === 
QIPs offer several benefits to companies. Firstly, it is a cost-effective way to raise capital as the process is relatively simpler and faster than a public issue. Secondly, it allows companies to raise capital without diluting the existing shareholdersâ€™ stake. Thirdly, it helps companies to raise capital from a wide range of investors, including foreign investors. Lastly, it helps companies to maintain their listing status as the process is relatively faster than a public issue.
===Eligibility Criteria for QIP === 
In order to be eligible for a QIP, companies must meet certain criteria. Firstly, the company must be listed on a recognized stock exchange in India. Secondly, the company must have a minimum net worth of Rs. 10 crore. Thirdly, the company must have a minimum public shareholding of 25%. Lastly, the company must have a minimum track record of three years of profitable operations.
===Process of QIP === 
The process of QIP involves several steps. Firstly, the company must obtain approval from the board of directors and the shareholders. Secondly, the company must obtain approval from the stock exchange. Thirdly, the company must appoint a merchant banker to manage the process. Fourthly, the company must file a draft offer document with the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI). Fifthly, the company must obtain approval from SEBI. Lastly, the company must issue the securities to the qualified institutional buyers.

In 2018, the Indian IT giant Wipro Ltd. raised Rs. 11,000 crore through a QIP. The company issued 1.2 crore equity shares at a price of Rs. 935 per share. The issue was subscribed by several institutional investors, including foreign investors.
