===Introduction to Marginal Benefit===

Marginal benefit is an economic concept that refers to the additional benefit that a consumer receives from consuming one additional unit of a good or service. It is a measure of the benefit that a consumer receives from consuming one additional unit of a good or service. It is used to determine the optimal level of consumption for a given good or service. It is also used to compare the benefits of different goods or services.
===Definition of Marginal Benefit===

Marginal benefit is defined as the additional benefit that a consumer receives from consuming one additional unit of a good or service.
