===Introduction to Walmart===

Walmart is one of the largest and most successful companies in the world. Founded in 1962 by Sam Walton, Walmart has grown to become the largest retailer in the world, with over 11,000 stores in 28 countries and e-commerce websites in 11 countries. Walmart is a multinational retail corporation that operates a chain of discount department stores, grocery stores, and hypermarkets. Walmart is the world’s largest company by revenue, with over $500 billion in annual revenue. Walmart is also the largest private employer in the world, with over 2.2 million employees. Walmart is a publicly traded company, listed on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol WMT.

===Walmart’s Business Model===

Walmart’s business model is based on providing low prices to customers. Walmart’s low prices are achieved through a combination of efficient operations, [[Economies Of Scale|economies of scale]], and strategic sourcing. Walmart’s efficient operations are achieved through the use of advanced technology, such as automated warehouses and self-checkout systems. Walmart’s economies of scale are achieved through the company’s large size, which allows it to purchase goods in bulk at lower prices. Walmart’s strategic sourcing is achieved through the company’s global [[Supply Chain|supply chain]], which allows it to source goods from around the world at lower prices.
