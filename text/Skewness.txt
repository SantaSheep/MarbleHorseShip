===Introduction to Skewness === 
Skewness is a measure of the asymmetry of a probability distribution. It is a measure of the degree to which a distribution is not symmetrical. Skewness is a measure of the degree to which a distribution deviates from a normal distribution. It is a measure of the degree to which the data is concentrated on one side of the mean. Skewness is an important concept in finance, as it can help investors and analysts to better understand the risk associated with a particular investment.
===Types of Skewness === 
There are two types of skewness: positive skewness and negative skewness. Positive skewness occurs when the data is concentrated on the right side of the mean. Negative skewness occurs when the data is concentrated on the left side of the mean.
===Causes of Skewness === 
Skewness can be caused by a variety of factors. These include outliers, non-normal distributions, and the presence of extreme values. Outliers are values that are far away from the mean. Non-normal distributions are distributions that are not symmetrical. Extreme values are values that are far away from the mean.
