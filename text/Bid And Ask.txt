===Introduction to Bid and Ask===

Bid and Ask is a term used in the [[Financial Market|financial market]]s to describe the two prices at which a security is traded. The bid price is the highest price that a buyer is willing to pay for a security, while the ask price is the lowest price that a seller is willing to accept for the security. The difference between the bid and ask prices is known as the bid-ask spread. Bid and Ask is an important concept in the financial markets, as it helps to determine the liquidity of a security and the cost of trading it.

===Examples of Bid and Ask===

For example, if a stock is trading at $50, the bid price may be $49.50 and the ask price may be $50.50. This means that a buyer is willing to pay $49.50 for the stock, while a seller is willing to accept $50.50 for the stock. The difference between the two prices is the bid-ask spread, which in this case is $1.
