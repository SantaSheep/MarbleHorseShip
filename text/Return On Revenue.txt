===Introduction to Return on Revenue===

Return on Revenue (ROR) is a financial metric used to measure the efficiency of a company’s operations. It is calculated by dividing the company’s [[Net Income|net income]] by its total revenue. ROR is a useful tool for investors and analysts to assess the profitability of a company and compare it to other companies in the same industry. ROR is also used to evaluate the performance of a company over time.
===Calculation of Return on Revenue===

ROR is calculated by dividing the company’s net income by its total revenue. Net income is the amount of money a company earns after subtracting all expenses, including taxes, from its total revenue. Total revenue is the total amount of money a company earns from its operations. To calculate ROR, the net income is divided by the total revenue and then multiplied by 100 to get the percentage.
===Benefits of Return on Revenue===

ROR is a useful tool for investors and analysts to assess the profitability of a company and compare it to other companies in the same industry. It is also used to evaluate the performance of a company over time. ROR can be used to identify companies that are more efficient and profitable than their competitors. It can also be used to identify companies that are underperforming and need to be improved.
===Limitations of Return on Revenue===

ROR is a useful tool, but it has some limitations. It does not take into account the company’s debt or other liabilities, which can affect the company’s profitability. It also does not take into account the company’s [[Cash Flow|cash flow]], which can be an important factor in assessing the company’s financial health. Additionally, ROR does not take into account the company’s long-term prospects, which can be important for investors.
