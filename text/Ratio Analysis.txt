===Introduction to Ratio Analysis===
Ratio analysis is a tool used by investors and financial analysts to evaluate the [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] of a company. It is a quantitative method of analyzing [[Financial Statements|financial statements]] to gain insight into a company’s financial health. Ratios are used to compare different aspects of a company’s financial performance, such as profitability, liquidity, and efficiency. By comparing ratios over time, investors and analysts can identify trends in a company’s financial performance and make informed decisions about investing in the company.
===Types of Ratios===
Ratios can be divided into two main categories: [[Profitability Ratios|profitability ratios]] and [[Liquidity Ratios|liquidity ratios]]. Profitability ratios measure a company’s ability to generate profits from its operations. Examples of profitability ratios include [[Return On Assets|return on assets]] (ROA), [[Return On Equity|return on equity]] (ROE), and [[Gross Profit|gross profit]] margin. Liquidity ratios measure a company’s ability to meet its short-term obligations. Examples of liquidity ratios include [[Current Ratio|current ratio]], [[Quick Ratio|quick ratio]], and [[Cash Ratio|cash ratio]].
===Uses of Ratio Analysis===
Ratio analysis can be used to assess a company’s financial performance in several ways. First, it can be used to compare a company’s performance to that of its competitors. By comparing ratios over time, investors and analysts can identify trends in a company’s financial performance and make informed decisions about investing in the company. Second, ratio analysis can be used to identify potential problems in a company’s financial performance. For example, a low return on assets ratio may indicate that the company is not generating enough profits from its operations. Finally, ratio analysis can be used to assess a company’s financial health. By comparing ratios over time, investors and analysts can identify potential problems in a company’s financial performance and make informed decisions about investing in the company.
===Limitations of Ratio Analysis===
Although ratio analysis is a useful tool for evaluating a company’s financial performance, it has several limitations. First, ratios are based on historical data and do not take into account future events or changes in the economy. Second, ratios are based [[On Account|on account]]ing data, which may be subject to manipulation. Finally, ratios do not take into account qualitative factors, such as a company’s competitive position or management quality.
===Conclusion===
Ratio analysis is a useful tool for evaluating a company’s financial performance. It can be used to compare a company’s performance to that of its competitors, identify potential problems in a company’s financial performance, and assess a company’s financial health. However, ratio analysis has several limitations, including its reliance on historical data, its susceptibility to manipulation, and its inability to take into account qualitative factors
