===History of Institute of Management Accountants===

IMA was founded in 1919 as the National Association of Cost Accountants (NACA). The organization was established to promote the practice of [[Cost Accounting|cost accounting]] and to provide a forum for the exchange of ideas and information among its members. In 1957, NACA changed its name to the Institute of Management Accountants (IMA). Since then, IMA has grown to become the world’s leading association for management accountants and financial professionals.

===Professional Certification from Institute of Management Accountants===

IMA offers two professional certifications: the Certified Management Accountant (CMA) and the Certified in Financial Management (CFM). The CMA is a globally recognized professional certification that demonstrates a mastery of financial planning, analysis, control, decision support, and professional ethics. The CFM is a specialized certification that focuses on financial management and is designed for financial professionals who want to demonstrate their expertise in the field.
