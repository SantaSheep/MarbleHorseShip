===Introduction to Profitability Ratios===

Profitability ratios are a set of financial metrics used to measure the ability of a company to generate profits from its operations. These ratios are used to assess the [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] of a company and to compare it to other companies in the same industry. They are also used to evaluate the efficiency of a company’s management in generating profits. The most commonly used profitability ratios are [[Gross Profit|gross profit]] margin, [[Operating Profit|operating profit]] margin, net [[Profit Margin|profit margin]], [[Return On Assets|return on assets]], and [[Return On Equity|return on equity]].
===Gross Profit Margin===

Gross profit margin is a profitability ratio that measures the percentage of revenue that is left after subtracting the cost of goods sold. It is calculated by dividing gross profit by total revenue. This ratio is used to measure the efficiency of a company’s production process and to compare it to other companies in the same industry. A higher gross profit margin indicates that a company is able to generate more profits from its operations.
===Operating Profit Margin===

Operating profit margin is a profitability ratio that measures the percentage of revenue that is left after subtracting all operating expenses. It is calculated by dividing operating profit by total revenue. This ratio is used to measure the efficiency of a company’s operations and to compare it to other companies in the same industry. A higher operating profit margin indicates that a company is able to generate more profits from its operations.
===Net Profit Margin===

Net profit margin is a profitability ratio that measures the percentage of revenue that is left after subtracting all expenses, including taxes. It is calculated by dividing net profit by total revenue. This ratio is used to measure the efficiency of a company’s operations and to compare it to other companies in the same industry. A higher net profit margin indicates that a company is able to generate more profits from its operations.
===Return on Assets===

Return on assets is a profitability ratio that measures the percentage of profits that are generated from a company’s total assets. It is calculated by dividing [[Net Income|net income]] by total assets. This ratio is used to measure the efficiency of a company’s management in generating profits from its assets and to compare it to other companies in the same industry. A higher return on assets indicates that a company is able to generate more profits from its assets.
===Return on Equity===

Return on equity is a profitability ratio that measures the percentage of profits that are generated from a company’s shareholders’ equity. It is calculated by dividing net income by shareholders’ equity. This ratio is used to measure the efficiency of a company’s management in generating profits from its equity and to compare it to other companies in the same industry. A higher return on equity indicates that a company is able to generate more profits from its equity.
