===Introduction to Cboe Global Markets===

Cboe Global Markets, Inc. (Cboe) is a global financial services company that provides trading and investment solutions to investors around the world. Cboe is the largest U.S. options exchange and the largest U.S. stock exchange by volume. Cboe offers a wide range of products and services, including options, futures, equities, and foreign exchange. Cboe also provides market data, analytics, and trading technology to its customers. Cboe is headquartered in Chicago, Illinois and has offices in New York, London, and Singapore.
===History of Cboe Global Markets===

Cboe was founded in 1973 as the Chicago Board Options Exchange (CBOE). The CBOE was the first U.S. options exchange and the first to list options on individual stocks. In 2005, the CBOE merged with the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT) to form the Cboe Global Markets. In 2007, Cboe acquired the International Securities Exchange (ISE), which was the first all-electronic options exchange in the U.S. In 2008, Cboe acquired the Boston Options Exchange (BOX), which was the first U.S. options exchange to offer an all-electronic trading platform. In 2017, Cboe acquired Bats Global Markets, which was the second-largest U.S. stock exchange by volume.
