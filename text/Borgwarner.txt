===Introduction to Borgwarner===

BorgWarner Inc. is a global automotive industry leader in powertrain solutions. The company designs, manufactures, and sells a wide range of products and services for the automotive industry, including turbochargers, engine timing systems, transmission components, and four-wheel drive systems. Founded in 1928, BorgWarner has grown to become one of the world’s largest automotive suppliers, with operations in more than 20 countries and over 30,000 employees worldwide. The company is headquartered in Auburn Hills, Michigan, and is listed on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE: BWA).
===History of Borgwarner===

BorgWarner was founded in 1928 by Charles Warner and his brother, John Warner, in Detroit, Michigan. The company began as a manufacturer of automotive parts, such as spark plugs and carburetors. In the 1950s, the company began to focus on the development of turbochargers, which would become its core business. In the 1960s, BorgWarner expanded its [[Product Line|product line]] to include engine timing systems, transmission components, and four-wheel drive systems.

In the 1970s, the company began to diversify its product line, entering into the aerospace and defense industries. In the 1980s, BorgWarner began to expand its operations internationally, opening plants in Europe, Asia, and South America. In the 1990s, the company continued to expand its global presence, and in 2000, it acquired the automotive components business of ITT Industries.
===Business Model of Borgwarner===

BorgWarner’s business model is focused on providing innovative powertrain solutions to the automotive industry. The company designs, manufactures, and sells a wide range of products and services for the automotive industry, including turbochargers, engine timing systems, transmission components, and four-wheel drive systems. The company also provides engineering services, such as design, development, and testing, to its customers.

BorgWarner’s products are sold to original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and aftermarket customers. The company’s customers include major automotive OEMs, such as Ford, [[General Motors|General Motors]], and Volkswagen, as well as aftermarket customers, such as independent repair shops and parts distributors.
