===Introduction to Boeing===

Boeing is an American [[Multinational Corporation|multinational corporation]] that designs, manufactures, and sells airplanes, rotorcraft, rockets, satellites, and missiles worldwide. It is the largest global aerospace and defense company and the largest exporter in the United States. Boeing is one of the world’s most recognizable brands and is a leader in the aerospace industry. The company has a long and storied history, and its [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] has been impressive over the years.
===History of Boeing===

Boeing was founded in 1916 by William Boeing in Seattle, Washington. The company initially focused on the production of seaplanes, but soon expanded into the production of commercial and military aircraft. During World War , Boeing was a major supplier of aircraft to the Allied forces. After the war, the company continued to grow and expand its [[Product Line|product line]]. In the 1950s, Boeing developed the first commercial jetliner, the 707, which revolutionized air travel. In the 1960s, Boeing developed the 747, the world’s first wide-body jetliner. In the 1970s, the company developed the first supersonic transport, the Concorde. In the 1980s, Boeing developed the first fly-by-wire aircraft, the 777. In the 1990s, Boeing developed the first commercial aircraft with a digital fly-by-wire system, the 787 Dreamliner.
===Financial Performance of Boeing===

Boeing has had a long history of strong financial performance. The company has consistently reported strong revenue and earnings growth over the years. In 2019, Boeing reported revenue of $76.6 billion and [[Net Income|net income]] of $10.5 billion. The company’s stock price has also been strong, with the stock price increasing from $75.00 per share in January 2019 to $372.00 per share in January 2021.
===Boeing’s Products and Services===

Boeing is a leader in the aerospace industry and produces a wide range of products and services. The company produces commercial and military aircraft, helicopters, satellites, missiles, and other aerospace products. Boeing also provides services such as aircraft maintenance, engineering, and training.
===Boeing’s Impact on the Economy===

Boeing is a major contributor to the U.S. economy. The company employs over 150,000 people in the United States and is one of the largest exporters in the country.
