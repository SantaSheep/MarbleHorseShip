===Introduction to Average Daily Rate===

Average Daily Rate (ADR) is a key metric used in the hospitality industry to measure the performance of a hotel or other lodging facility. It is a measure of the average rate paid for a room per night, and is used to compare the performance of different hotels and to track the performance of a single hotel over time. ADR is an important indicator of a hotel's financial health, as it is a measure of the amount of revenue generated per room.
===Definition of Average Daily Rate===

Average Daily Rate (ADR) is a measure of the average rate paid for a room per night. It is calculated by dividing the total room revenue for a given period of time by the total number of rooms sold during that period. ADR is used to compare the performance of different hotels and to track the performance of a single hotel over time.
===Calculation of Average Daily Rate===

Average Daily Rate (ADR) is calculated by dividing the total room revenue for a given period of time by the total number of rooms sold during that period. For example, if a hotel had total room revenue of $100,000 and sold 1,000 rooms during the period, the ADR would be $100.
===Examples of Average Daily Rate===

Average Daily Rate (ADR) is used to compare the performance of different hotels and to track the performance of a single hotel over time. For example, if two hotels have the same total room revenue but one hotel sold more rooms, the hotel with the higher ADR would be considered to be performing better. Similarly, if a hotel's ADR increases over time, it indicates that the hotel is doing better financially.
===Advantages and Disadvantages of Average Daily Rate===

Average Daily Rate (ADR) is a useful metric for measuring the performance of a hotel or other lodging facility. It is a simple and straightforward measure that can be used to compare the performance of different hotels and to track the performance of a single hotel over time. However, it is important to note that ADR does not take into account other factors such as occupancy rate or length of stay, which can also affect a hotel's [[Financial Performance|financial performance]].
