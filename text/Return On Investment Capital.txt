===Introduction to Return on Investment Capital===

[[Return On Investment|Return on Investment]] Capital (ROIC) is a financial metric used to measure the efficiency of a company’s capital investments. It is a measure of how much profit a company is able to generate from its investments in [[Capital Asset|capital asset]]s. ROIC is an important metric for investors, as it can help them determine the potential return on their investments. ROIC is also used by companies to assess the effectiveness of their capital investments and to make decisions about future investments.
===Definition of Return on Investment Capital===

Return on Investment Capital (ROIC) is a financial metric that measures the efficiency of a company’s capital investments. It is calculated by dividing the company’s net [[Operating Profit|operating profit]] after taxes (NOPAT) by its total [[Invested Capital|invested capital]] (TIC). The result is expressed as a percentage. A higher ROIC indicates that the company is able to generate more profit from its investments in capital assets.
===Calculation of Return on Investment Capital===

The calculation of Return on Investment Capital (ROIC) is relatively straightforward. It is calculated by dividing the company’s net operating profit after taxes (NOPAT) by its total invested capital (TIC). The result is expressed as a percentage.

NOPAT = Operating Profit – Taxes

TIC = Total Assets – Non-Interest Bearing Liabilities

ROIC = NOPAT / TIC
