===Introduction to the Durbin Watson Statistic===

The Durbin Watson statistic is a statistical measure used to detect autocorrelation in a [[Time Series|time series]]. Autocorrelation is the correlation of a variable with itself over different time periods. The Durbin Watson statistic is used to detect the presence of autocorrelation in a time series and to measure the strength of the autocorrelation.
===Definition of the Durbin Watson Statistic===

The Durbin Watson statistic is a measure of autocorrelation in a time series. It is calculated by taking the sum of the squared differences between consecutive observations in the time series and dividing it by the sum of the squared differences between the mean of the time series and each observation. The result is a number between 0 and 4, with 0 indicating no autocorrelation and 4 indicating perfect autocorrelation.
