===Introduction to Operating Revenue===

Operating revenue is a term used to describe the total amount of money a business earns from its normal operations. It is the primary source of income for a business and is used to measure the success of a business. Operating revenue is also known as [[Gross Income|gross income]], [[Gross Profit|gross profit]], or [[Net Sales|net sales]]. Operating revenue is the total amount of money a business earns from its normal operations before any deductions for expenses, taxes, or other costs. It is the primary source of income for a business and is used to measure the success of a business.
===Components of Operating Revenue===

Operating revenue is composed of several components. These components include sales revenue, cost of goods sold, and operating expenses. Sales revenue is the total amount of money a business earns from the sale of its products or services. Cost of goods sold is the total cost of the goods or services sold by the business. Operating expenses are the costs associated with running the business, such as rent, utilities, salaries, and other overhead costs.
===Calculating Operating Revenue===

Operating revenue is calculated by subtracting the cost of goods sold and operating expenses from the total sales revenue. This calculation is known as the gross [[Profit Margin|profit margin]]. The gross profit margin is the difference between the total sales revenue and the total cost of goods sold and operating expenses.
===Advantages of Operating Revenue===

Operating revenue is an important measure of a business’s success. It is used to measure the profitability of a business and to compare the performance of different businesses. Operating revenue is also used to determine the amount of taxes a business must pay.
===Disadvantages of Operating Revenue===

One of the disadvantages of operating revenue is that it does not take into account the [[Cost Of Capital|cost of capital]]. Capital costs are the costs associated with borrowing money to finance a business’s operations. These costs can have a significant impact on a business’s profitability and should be taken into account when calculating operating revenue.
