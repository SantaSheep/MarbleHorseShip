===Introduction to AT&T===

AT&T Inc. is an American multinational telecommunications conglomerate headquartered in Dallas, Texas. It is the world’s largest telecommunications company, the second largest provider of mobile telephone services, and the largest provider of fixed telephone services in the United States. AT&T is the [[Parent Company|parent company]] of WarnerMedia, one of the world’s largest media and entertainment conglomerates. AT&T is also the parent company of DirecTV, a leading provider of digital television entertainment services in the United States.
===History of AT&T===

AT&T was founded in 1885 as the American Telephone and Telegraph Company. It was the result of the merger of several smaller companies, including Bell Telephone Company, Western Union Telegraph Company, and American Bell Telephone Company. AT&T quickly became the largest telecommunications company in the United States, and by the early 20th century, it had become the largest telephone company in the world.

In the early 20th century, AT&T began to expand its business beyond telephone services. It acquired several companies, including Western Electric, which manufactured telephones and other telecommunications equipment, and Bell Laboratories, which conducted research and development in telecommunications. AT&T also acquired several regional telephone companies, which allowed it to expand its service area.
