===Introduction to Hershey’s===

Hershey’s is one of the most iconic and beloved brands in the world. Founded in 1894 by Milton S. Hershey, the company has grown to become one of the largest and most successful confectionery companies in the world. Hershey’s is best known for its iconic chocolate bars, but the company also produces a wide variety of other products, including candy, ice cream, and baking products. The company is headquartered in Hershey, Pennsylvania, and has operations in more than 80 countries around the world. Hershey’s is a publicly traded company and is listed on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) under the ticker symbol HSY.
===History of Hershey’s===

Milton S. Hershey founded the Hershey Chocolate Company in 1894 in Lancaster, Pennsylvania. The company initially produced caramels, but Hershey soon realized that chocolate was a more profitable product. In 1900, Hershey opened the Hershey Chocolate Factory in Derry Township, Pennsylvania, and began producing chocolate bars. The company quickly grew in popularity and by the 1920s, Hershey’s was the largest chocolate manufacturer in the United States.

In the 1950s, Hershey’s began to expand its [[Product Line|product line]] to include other confectionery products, such as ice cream and baking products. The company also began to expand internationally, opening factories in Canada, Mexico, and the United Kingdom. In the 1980s, Hershey’s acquired several other confectionery companies, including Peter Paul and Reese’s. Today, Hershey’s is one of the largest confectionery companies in the world.
