===Introduction to Gartner===
Gartner is a leading research and advisory company that provides insights, advice, and tools to help business and IT leaders make the right decisions. Founded in 1979, Gartner has grown to become a global leader in providing technology-related research and advice to organizations of all sizes. Gartner’s services include research, consulting, events, and training. Gartner’s clients include Fortune 500 companies, government agencies, and small and medium-sized businesses.
===History of Gartner===
Gartner was founded in 1979 by Gideon Gartner and his wife, Esther Gartner. The company was initially focused on providing technology-related research and advice to organizations. Over the years, Gartner has grown to become a global leader in providing technology-related research and advice to organizations of all sizes. Gartner has grown to become a publicly traded company and is listed on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE: IT).
===Gartner’s Financial Performance===
Gartner’s [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] has been strong over the past few years. In 2020, Gartner reported total revenue of $4.3 billion, an increase of 8.3% from the previous year. ===Gartner’s Business Model===
Gartner’s business model is based on providing research, consulting, events, and training services to its clients. Gartner’s research services include [[Market Research|market research]], competitive intelligence, and technology trends. Gartner’s consulting services include strategy and operations consulting, technology consulting, and digital transformation consulting. Gartner’s events include conferences, webinars, and virtual events. Gartner’s training services include online and in-person training.
