===Introduction to Owner Earnings Run Rate === 
Owner Earnings Run Rate (OERR) is a financial metric used to measure the profitability of a company. It is calculated by taking the company’s net income and adding back any non-cash expenses such as depreciation and amortization. This metric is used to assess the company’s ability to generate cash flow from its operations. OERR is a useful tool for investors and analysts to evaluate the financial health of a company.
