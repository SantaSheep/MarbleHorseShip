===Introduction to Harvard Business===

Harvard Business is a term used to refer to the Harvard Business School, which is the graduate business school of Harvard University. Founded in 1908, Harvard Business School is one of the most prestigious business schools in the world and is renowned for its rigorous academic standards and its commitment to developing leaders who make a difference in the world. Harvard Business School offers a variety of degree programs, including MBA, Executive Education, and Doctoral programs, as well as a range of research and teaching initiatives.
===History of Harvard Business===

Harvard Business School was founded in 1908 by a group of prominent Boston businessmen, led by Harvard alumnus and former U.S. Secretary of the Treasury, George F. Baker. The school was established to provide a practical education in business and management to prepare students for the challenges of the modern business world. Since its founding, Harvard Business School has grown to become one of the most prestigious business schools in the world, with a global network of alumni and faculty.
===Types of Harvard Business===

Harvard Business School offers a variety of degree programs, including the MBA, Executive Education, and Doctoral programs. The Executive Education program is designed for experienced professionals who want to further their knowledge and skills in a particular area of business. The Doctoral program is designed for those who wish to pursue a career in academia or research.
