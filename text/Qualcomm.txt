===Introduction to Qualcomm===
Qualcomm Incorporated is a global leader in the development and commercialization of advanced wireless technologies and products. Founded in 1985, Qualcomm is headquartered in San Diego, California and has operations in more than 150 countries. Qualcomm is a publicly traded company on the NASDAQ stock exchange and is a component of the S&P 500 index. Qualcomm is a leader in the development of mobile technologies, including 3G, 4G, and 5G cellular networks, as well as the development of mobile processors, modems, and other components for mobile devices. Qualcomm is also a leader in the development of mobile applications, such as mobile payments, mobile health, and mobile gaming.
===History of Qualcomm===
Qualcomm was founded in 1985 by Irwin Jacobs, Andrew Viterbi, and Harvey White. The company was initially focused on the development of digital cellular technology, which was then in its infancy. Qualcomm developed the first commercial CDMA cellular network in the United States in 1995. This technology was quickly adopted by other cellular providers and became the standard for cellular networks in the United States. Qualcomm has since become a leader in the development of 3G, 4G, and 5G cellular networks, as well as the development of mobile processors, modems, and other components for mobile devices.
===Qualcomm’s Business Model===
Qualcomm’s business model is based on the licensing of its intellectual property. Qualcomm owns a portfolio of patents related to wireless technologies, which it licenses to other companies. Qualcomm also licenses its technology to device manufacturers, such as Apple and Samsung, who use Qualcomm’s technology in their products. Qualcomm also sells chipsets and other components to device manufacturers. In addition, Qualcomm provides services such as engineering and software development to device manufacturers.
