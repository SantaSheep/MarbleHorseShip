===Introduction to Economic Capital === 
Economic capital is a measure of a company’s financial strength and ability to absorb losses. It is a key component of risk management and is used to assess the potential impact of a company’s financial decisions on its overall financial health. Economic capital is a measure of the amount of capital a company has available to cover potential losses from its operations. It is a key factor in determining the amount of risk a company is willing to take on and the amount of capital it needs to maintain its operations.
===Definition of Economic Capital === 
Economic capital is defined as the amount of capital a company has available to cover potential losses from its operations. It is a measure of the company’s financial strength and ability to absorb losses. It is a key component of risk management and is used to assess the potential impact of a company’s financial decisions on its overall financial health.
===Components of Economic Capital === 
The components of economic capital include the company’s assets, liabilities, and equity. Assets are the resources owned by the company, such as cash, investments, and property. Liabilities are the debts and obligations of the company, such as loans and accounts payable. Equity is the difference between the company’s assets and liabilities.
===Calculating Economic Capital === 
Economic capital is calculated by subtracting the company’s liabilities from its assets. This calculation is used to determine the amount of capital a company has available to cover potential losses from its operations.
===Benefits of Economic Capital === 
The benefits of economic capital include improved risk management, increased financial stability, and improved decision-making. Economic capital helps companies assess the potential impact of their financial decisions on their overall financial health. It also helps companies identify potential risks and develop strategies to mitigate them.
===Example of Economic Capital in Practice=== 
An example of economic capital in practice is a company that has $10 million in assets and $5 million in liabilities. The company’s economic capital is $5 million, which is the difference between its assets and liabilities. This amount of economic capital can be used to cover potential losses from its operations.
