===Introduction to Gini Index === 
The Gini Index is a measure of inequality in a population. It is used to measure the distribution of income or wealth among individuals or households in a given population. The Gini Index is a number between 0 and 1, where 0 represents perfect equality and 1 represents perfect inequality. The higher the Gini Index, the greater the inequality in the population.
===History of Gini Index === 
The Gini Index was first developed by Italian statistician Corrado Gini in 1912. Gini was interested in measuring the degree of inequality in a population and developed the Gini Index as a way to measure this. Since then, the Gini Index has been used in many different fields, including economics, sociology, and public policy.
===How Gini Index is Calculated === 
The Gini Index is calculated by taking the cumulative share of the population and plotting it against the cumulative share of income or wealth.
