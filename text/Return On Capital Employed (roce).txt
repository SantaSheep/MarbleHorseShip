===Introduction to Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) === 
Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) is a financial metric used to measure the efficiency of a company’s use of its capital. It is a key indicator of a company’s financial health and performance, and is used to compare the performance of different companies. ROCE is a popular metric among investors, as it provides insight into the company’s ability to generate profits from its capital.
===What is Return on Capital Employed (ROCE)? === 
Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) is a financial ratio that measures the efficiency of a company’s use of its capital. It is calculated by dividing the company’s operating profit by its total capital employed. ROCE is expressed as a percentage and is used to compare the performance of different companies.
===How to Calculate Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) === 
Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) is calculated by dividing the company’s operating profit by its total capital employed. Operating profit is calculated by subtracting operating expenses from total revenue. Total capital employed is calculated by adding the company’s total assets to its total liabilities.
===Benefits of Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) === 
Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) is a useful metric for investors, as it provides insight into the company’s ability to generate profits from its capital. It is also a useful tool for comparing the performance of different companies. ROCE can be used to identify companies that are using their capital efficiently and are likely to generate higher returns in the future.
===Limitations of Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) === 
Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) is a useful metric, but it has some limitations. It does not take into account the company’s debt levels, which can have a significant impact on the company’s performance. Additionally, ROCE does not take into account the company’s cash flow, which can also have an impact on the company’s performance.
