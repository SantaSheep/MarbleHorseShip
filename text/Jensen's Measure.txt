===Introduction to Jensen's Measure === 
Jensen's Measure is a financial metric used to measure the performance of a portfolio relative to a benchmark index. It was developed by Michael Jensen in 1968 and is also known as the Jensen Index or the Jensen Alpha. It is used to measure the excess return of a portfolio over and above the return of a benchmark index. The Jensen Measure is a widely used tool in the financial industry and is used to evaluate the performance of a portfolio manager.
===Overview of Jensen's Alpha ===
