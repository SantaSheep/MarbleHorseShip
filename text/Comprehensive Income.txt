===Introduction to Comprehensive Income===
Comprehensive income is a financial term that refers to the total change in a company’s equity during a given period of time. It is a measure of the company’s [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] and includes all changes in equity, both realized and unrealized. Comprehensive income is a broader measure of a company’s financial performance than [[Net Income|net income]], which only includes realized gains and losses. Comprehensive income is also known as total comprehensive income or total comprehensive loss.
===Components of Comprehensive Income===
Comprehensive income is composed of two components: net income and other comprehensive income. Net income is the total amount of income earned by a company during a given period of time, minus any expenses and taxes. Other comprehensive income is the total amount of income earned by a company during a given period of time, minus any expenses and taxes, but also includes unrealized gains and losses. Unrealized gains and losses are changes in the value of assets and liabilities that have not yet been realized. Examples of unrealized gains and losses include changes in the value of investments, foreign currency [[Exchange Rate|exchange rate]] changes, and changes in the value of derivatives.
===Accounting Treatment of Comprehensive Income===
The accounting treatment of comprehensive income depends on the type of company. For publicly traded companies, comprehensive income is reported on the [[Income Statement|income statement]]. For private companies, comprehensive income is reported on the statement of changes in equity. Comprehensive income is also reported on the [[Balance Sheet|balance sheet]] as a separate line item.
===Reporting Comprehensive Income===
Comprehensive income is reported in the [[Financial Statements|financial statements]] of a company. It is reported on the income statement, the statement of changes in equity, and the balance sheet. Comprehensive income is reported in the financial statements of a company in order to provide investors and other stakeholders with a more comprehensive view of the company’s financial performance.
===Advantages and Disadvantages of Comprehensive Income===
The main advantage of comprehensive income is that it provides a more comprehensive view of a company’s financial performance. It includes both realized and unrealized gains and losses, which gives investors and other stakeholders a better understanding of the company’s financial position. The main disadvantage of comprehensive income is that it can be difficult to understand and interpret.
