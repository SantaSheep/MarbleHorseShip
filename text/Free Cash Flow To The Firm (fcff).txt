===Introduction to Free Cash Flow to the Firm (FCFF) === 
Free Cash Flow to the Firm (FCFF) is a measure of a company’s financial performance that takes into account the cash generated from operations, minus capital expenditures and taxes. It is a measure of the cash available to the firm to pay dividends, buy back stock, pay down debt, or reinvest in the business. FCFF is a key metric used by investors and analysts to evaluate a company’s financial health and performance.
===Overview of FCFF Calculation === 
The calculation of FCFF is relatively straightforward. It is calculated by taking the company’s net income, adding back non-cash expenses such as depreciation and amortization, subtracting capital expenditures, and then subtracting taxes. This calculation results in the company’s free cash flow to the firm.
===Benefits of FCFF === 
The primary benefit of FCFF is that it provides a more accurate picture of a company’s financial performance than other measures such as net income. By taking into account capital expenditures and taxes, FCFF provides a more accurate measure of the cash available to the firm. This is important for investors and analysts who are looking to evaluate a company’s financial health and performance.
===Limitations of FCFF === 
One of the primary limitations of FCFF is that it does not take into account the company’s debt obligations. This means that it does not provide a complete picture of the company’s financial health. Additionally, FCFF does not take into account the company’s future cash flows, which can be important for investors and analysts.
