===Introduction to Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) === 
Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) is a term used to describe a person’s income for the purpose of determining eligibility for certain tax credits and deductions. It is calculated by taking a person’s Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) and adding back certain deductions that were taken in the calculation of AGI. MAGI is used to determine eligibility for tax credits such as the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) and the Child Tax Credit (CTC).
===How MAGI is Calculated === 
MAGI is calculated by taking a person’s Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) and adding back certain deductions that were taken in the calculation of AGI. These deductions include student loan interest, tuition and fees, and foreign earned income exclusion. The resulting number is the Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI).
