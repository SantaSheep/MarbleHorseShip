===Introduction to Faang Stocks===
FANG stocks, also known as FAANG stocks, are a group of five popular technology stocks that have been widely discussed in the financial world. The acronym stands for Facebook, Amazon, Apple, Netflix, and Google (Alphabet). These stocks have been some of the most successful investments in the past decade, and have become a popular investment choice for many investors.
===History of Faang Stocks===
The FANG stocks were first identified in 2013 by CNBC’s Jim Cramer, who noted that these five stocks had outperformed the S&P 500 index by a wide margin. Since then, the FANG stocks have become a popular investment choice for many investors. The stocks have continued to outperform the S&P 500 index, and have become a major part of many investors’ portfolios.
