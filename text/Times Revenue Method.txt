===Introduction to Times Revenue Method===
The Times Revenue Method is a [[Financial Analysis|financial analysis]] technique used to evaluate the performance of a company over a period of time. It is a useful tool for investors and analysts to assess the financial health of a company and to make informed decisions about investing in the company. The Times Revenue Method is based on the concept of “times revenue”, which is the ratio of a company’s total revenue to its total assets. This ratio is used to measure the efficiency of a company’s operations and to compare it to other companies in the same industry.
===Overview of Times Revenue Method===
The Times Revenue Method is a financial analysis technique that uses the ratio of a company’s total revenue to its total assets to measure the efficiency of a company’s operations. This ratio is calculated by dividing the company’s total revenue by its total assets. The higher the ratio, the more efficient the company’s operations are. The Times Revenue Method is used to compare the performance of a company to other companies in the same industry.

===Disadvantages of Times Revenue Method===
