===Introduction to Fifth Third Bank===

Fifth Third Bank is a regional banking corporation headquartered in Cincinnati, Ohio. It is the principal subsidiary of Fifth Third Bancorp, a bank [[Holding Company|holding company]]. Fifth Third Bank operates 1,154 branches and 2,469 ATMs in Ohio, Kentucky, Indiana, Michigan, Illinois, Florida, Tennessee, West Virginia, Georgia, and North Carolina. It is one of the largest money managers in the Midwest and, as of June 30, 2020, had $179 billion in assets. Fifth Third Bank offers a wide range of banking, investment, and insurance products and services to its customers.
===History of Fifth Third Bank===

Fifth Third Bank traces its roots back to 1858, when the Bank of the Ohio Valley opened its doors in Cincinnati. In 1871, the bank changed its name to the Third National Bank of Cincinnati. In 1908, the bank merged with the Fifth National Bank of Cincinnati and became the Fifth Third Bank. The bank continued to grow and expand its operations throughout the 20th century, and in 1998, it became a publicly traded company.
===Products and Services Offered by Fifth Third Bank===

Fifth Third Bank offers a wide range of banking, investment, and insurance products and services to its customers. These include checking and savings accounts, [[Credit Card|credit card]]s, mortgages, auto loans, personal loans, [[Business Banking|business banking]], and [[Wealth Management|wealth management]] services. The bank also offers online and mobile banking services, as well as a variety of other services such as bill pay, money transfers, and mobile deposits.
===Financial Performance of Fifth Third Bank===

Fifth Third Bank has had a strong [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] in recent years. In 2020, the bank reported [[Net Income|net income]] of $2.3 billion, up from $1.9 billion in 2019. The bank’s total assets increased from $179 billion in 2019 to $183 billion in 2020. The bank’s return on average assets was 1.27%, and its return on average equity was 11.2%.
