===Types of Volatility===

Volatility can be divided into two main types: historical volatility and implied volatility. Historical volatility is a measure of the past price movements of a security or asset. It is calculated by taking the [[Standard Deviation|standard deviation]] of the security or asset’s price over a given period of time. Implied volatility is a measure of the expected future price movements of a security or asset. It is calculated by taking the implied volatility of the options associated with the security or asset.
===Measuring Volatility===

Volatility can be measured using a variety of methods. The most common method is to calculate the standard deviation of the security or asset’s price over a given period of time. This is known as historical volatility. Other methods of measuring volatility include the use of options-based measures such as implied volatility and the VIX index.
