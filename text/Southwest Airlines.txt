===Introduction to Southwest Airlines===
Southwest Airlines is an American low-cost airline headquartered in Dallas, Texas. It is the world’s largest low-cost carrier, operating more than 4,000 flights daily to more than 100 destinations in the United States and nine other countries. Southwest Airlines is known for its low fares, friendly [[Customer Service|customer service]], and its commitment to providing a safe and reliable travel experience. The company has been in operation since 1971 and has grown to become one of the most successful airlines in the world.
===History of Southwest Airlines===
Southwest Airlines was founded in 1971 by Rollin King and Herb Kelleher. The company began operations with three Boeing 737 aircraft, serving three cities in Texas. Over the years, the company has grown to become one of the largest airlines in the world, with a fleet of more than 700 aircraft and serving more than 100 destinations. Southwest Airlines has also become known for its innovative approach to customer service, offering a variety of amenities such as free snacks and drinks, free Wi-Fi, and a loyalty program.
===Financial Performance of Southwest Airlines===
Southwest Airlines has been consistently profitable since its inception in 1971. In 2019, the company reported a [[Net Income|net income]] of $2.7 billion on total revenues of $21.2 billion. The company’s [[Operating Margin|operating margin]] was 12.7%, and its [[Return On Equity|return on equity]] was 17.2%. Southwest Airlines has also been able to maintain a strong [[Balance Sheet|balance sheet]], with total assets of $37.2 billion and [[Total Liabilities|total liabilities]] of $25.2 billion.
