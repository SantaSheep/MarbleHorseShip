===Introduction to Defensive Interval Ratio===

The Defensive Interval Ratio (DIR) is a financial ratio used to measure the liquidity of a company. It is calculated by dividing the company’s [[Current Assets|current assets]] by its [[Current Liabilities|current liabilities]]. The DIR is used to assess the ability of a company to meet its short-term obligations. It is also used to compare the liquidity of different companies in the same industry.
