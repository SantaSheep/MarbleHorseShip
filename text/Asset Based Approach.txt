===Benefits of Asset-Based Approach === 
Examples of Asset-Based Approach - Asset-based approach can be used to assess the value of a company’s assets, such as cash, inventory, and equipment. It can also be used to evaluate the company’s ability to generate cash flow. For example, a company’s cash flow can be evaluated by analyzing its accounts receivable and accounts payable.

===Challenges of Asset-Based Approach === 
Conclusion - Asset-based approach is a financial strategy that focuses on the value of a company’s assets to determine its financial health. This approach provides a comprehensive view of a company’s financial health and can be used to identify potential risks and opportunities for improvement. However, it can be difficult to implement, as it requires a thorough understanding of the company’s assets and liabilities.
