===Introduction to Cash and Cash Equivalents (CCE) === 

===Types of CCE === 
Accounting Treatment of CCE: Cash and cash equivalents are reported on the balance sheet as current assets. They are reported at their fair market value, which is the amount that could be received if the asset were sold in an orderly transaction.

===Advantages of CCE === 
Conclusion: Cash and cash equivalents are a valuable source of short-term financing for businesses. They are highly liquid and can be quickly converted into cash. However, they are not a good source of long-term financing and carry a risk of loss due to inflation or market fluctuations.
