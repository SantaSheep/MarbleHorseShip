===Introduction to Efficiency Ratio===

The efficiency ratio is a financial metric used to measure the efficiency of a company’s operations. It is calculated by dividing a company’s operating expenses by its total revenue. The efficiency ratio is used to assess the company’s ability to generate profits from its operations. It is also used to compare the efficiency of different companies in the same industry.
===Types of Efficiency Ratios===

There are several types of efficiency ratios that can be used to measure a company’s efficiency. These include the operating [[Expense Ratio|expense ratio]], the [[Asset Turnover|asset turnover]] ratio, the [[Inventory Turnover|inventory turnover]] ratio, and the [[Accounts Receivable|accounts receivable]] turnover ratio. Each of these ratios measures different aspects of a company’s operations and can be used to assess the efficiency of a company’s operations.
===Calculating Efficiency Ratios===

The efficiency ratio is calculated by dividing a company’s operating expenses by its total revenue. The operating expenses include all costs associated with running the business, such as salaries, rent, and utilities. The total revenue includes all income generated by the company, such as sales, interest, and dividends.
===Interpreting Efficiency Ratios===

The efficiency ratio is used to assess the efficiency of a company’s operations. A higher efficiency ratio indicates that the company is able to generate more profits from its operations. A lower efficiency ratio indicates that the company is not as efficient in generating profits from its operations.
