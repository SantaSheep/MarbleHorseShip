===Challenges of Multi-Factor Modeling === 
Multi-factor models can be used to analyze a variety of investments, including stocks, bonds, and mutual funds. For example, a multi-factor model might be used to analyze the performance of a stock by taking into account factors such as the company’s earnings, its price-to-earnings ratio, and its dividend yield. Similarly, a multi-factor model might be used to analyze the performance of a bond by taking into account factors such as the issuer’s credit rating, the bond’s maturity date, and its coupon rate.
