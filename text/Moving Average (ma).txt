===Introduction to Moving Average (MA) === 
Moving Average (MA) is a technical analysis tool used to identify trends in financial markets. It is a type of lagging indicator, meaning it follows the price action of a security or index. MA is calculated by taking the average of a certain number of past prices, usually closing prices, and plotting them on a chart. This helps to smooth out the price action and identify the overall trend of the security or index.
===Types of Moving Averages === 
There are several types of Moving Averages, including Simple Moving Average (SMA), Exponential Moving Average (EMA), Weighted Moving Average (WMA), and Hull Moving Average (HMA). Each type of MA has its own calculation and is used for different purposes.
