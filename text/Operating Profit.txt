===Introduction to Operating Profit===

Operating profit is a measure of a company’s [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] that is used to assess the profitability of a business. It is calculated by subtracting operating expenses from total revenue. Operating profit is an important metric for investors and analysts to consider when evaluating a company’s financial health. It is also used to compare the performance of different companies in the same industry.
===Definition of Operating Profit===

Operating profit is defined as the amount of money a company earns from its core business operations after deducting all operating expenses. Operating expenses include costs such as wages, rent, utilities, and other costs associated with running the business. Operating profit does not include non-[[Operating Income|operating income]] such as interest income or gains from investments.
===Components of Operating Profit===

Operating profit is composed of two components: [[Gross Profit|gross profit]] and operating expenses. Gross profit is the amount of money a company earns from its core business operations after deducting the cost of goods sold. Operating expenses are the costs associated with running the business, such as wages, rent, utilities, and other costs.
===Calculating Operating Profit===

Operating profit is calculated by subtracting operating expenses from total revenue. The formula for calculating operating profit is: Operating Profit = Total Revenue – Operating Expenses.
===Advantages of Operating Profit===

Operating profit is an important metric for investors and analysts to consider when evaluating a company’s financial health. It is a measure of a company’s profitability and can be used to compare the performance of different companies in the same industry. Operating profit is also a useful tool for assessing the efficiency of a company’s operations.
===Disadvantages of Operating Profit===

One of the main disadvantages of operating profit is that it does not include non-operating income such as interest income or gains from investments. This means that it does not provide a complete picture of a company’s financial performance. Additionally, operating profit does not take into account the [[Cost Of Capital|cost of capital]], which can have a significant impact on a company’s profitability.
