===Introduction to Operating Earnings===

Operating earnings, also known as [[Operating Income|operating income]], is a measure of a company’s profitability that excludes non-operating items such as interest income, taxes, and [[Extraordinary Item|extraordinary item]]s. Operating earnings are a key metric used by investors and analysts to evaluate a company’s performance and financial health. Operating earnings are a more accurate measure of a company’s performance than [[Net Income|net income]], which includes non-operating items.
===Definition of Operating Earnings===

Operating earnings are defined as the net income generated from a company’s core operations, excluding non-operating items such as interest income, taxes, and extraordinary items. Operating earnings are calculated by subtracting operating expenses from [[Operating Revenue|operating revenue]]s. Operating earnings are also referred to as operating income, [[Operating Profit|operating profit]], or EBIT (earnings before interest and taxes).
===Components of Operating Earnings===

Operating earnings are composed of two components: operating revenues and operating expenses. Operating revenues are the revenues generated from a company’s core operations, such as sales of goods and services. Operating expenses are the costs associated with running a business, such as salaries, rent, and utilities.
===Uses of Operating Earnings===

Operating earnings are used by investors and analysts to evaluate a company’s performance and financial health. Operating earnings are a more accurate measure of a company’s performance than net income, which includes non-operating items. Operating earnings can be used to compare a company’s performance to that of its peers, as well as to assess the company’s ability to generate profits from its core operations.
===Advantages of Operating Earnings===

Operating earnings are a more accurate measure of a company’s performance than net income, which includes non-operating items. Operating earnings are also useful for comparing a company’s performance to that of its peers, as well as for assessing the company’s ability to generate profits from its core operations.
===Disadvantages of Operating Earnings===

The main disadvantage of operating earnings is that they exclude non-operating items such as interest income, taxes, and extraordinary items. This means that operating earnings may not provide a complete picture of a company’s financial health. Additionally, operating earnings may be subject to manipulation by management, as they can be adjusted to exclude certain expenses or include certain revenues.
