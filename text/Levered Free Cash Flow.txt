===Introduction to Levered Free Cash Flow===

Levered free [[Cash Flow|cash flow]] (LFCF) is a financial metric used to measure the amount of cash available to a company after taking into account its debt obligations. It is a measure of a company’s financial health and is used to assess the company’s ability to pay its debt and other financial obligations. LFCF is an important metric for investors and creditors as it provides an indication of the company’s ability to generate cash flow and pay its debts.
===Definition of Levered Free Cash Flow===

Levered [[Free Cash Flow|free cash flow]] is defined as the amount of cash available to a company after taking into account its debt obligations. It is calculated by subtracting the company’s total debt payments from its [[Operating Cash Flow|operating cash flow]]. LFCF is a measure of a company’s financial health and is used to assess the company’s ability to pay its debt and other financial obligations.
===Calculation of Levered Free Cash Flow===

Levered free cash flow is calculated by subtracting the company’s total debt payments from its operating cash flow. The formula for calculating LFCF is as follows:

LFCF = Operating Cash Flow – Total Debt Payments

The operating cash flow is the amount of cash generated by the company’s operations. The total debt payments include all payments made by the company to service its debt, such as interest payments, principal payments, and other debt-related expenses.
===Advantages of Levered Free Cash Flow===

Levered free cash flow is a useful metric for investors and creditors as it provides an indication of the company’s ability to generate cash flow and pay its debts. It is also a useful tool for assessing the company’s financial health and its ability to meet its financial obligations.
===Disadvantages of Levered Free Cash Flow===

The main disadvantage of levered free cash flow is that it does not take into account the company’s future cash flows. It is also important to note that LFCF does not take into account the company’s [[Capital Structure|capital structure]], which can have a significant impact on the company’s ability to generate cash flow and pay its debts.
