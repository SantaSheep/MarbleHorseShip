===Introduction to Verizon===
Verizon Communications Inc. is an American multinational telecommunications conglomerate and a corporate component of the Dow Jones Industrial Average. It is the largest U.S. wireless communications service provider, as well as the largest U.S. provider of fixed telephone services. Verizon is the second-largest telecommunications company in the world by [[Market Capitalization|market capitalization]], after China Mobile. The company is headquartered in New York City and has operations in over 150 countries.
===History of Verizon===
Verizon was founded in 2000 as a result of the merger of Bell Atlantic and GTE. Bell Atlantic was formed in 1983 as a result of the breakup of the Bell System, while GTE was formed in 1918. The two companies had a long history of working together, and the merger was seen as a natural progression.
===Verizon’s Business Model===
Verizon’s business model is based on providing wireless and wireline communications services to consumers, businesses, and government entities. The company offers a wide range of services, including voice, data, and video services, as well as internet access and cloud-based services. Verizon also provides a variety of products and services, such as mobile phones, tablets, and home phone services.
