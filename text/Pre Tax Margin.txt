===Introduction to Pretax Margin===

Pretax margin is a financial metric used to measure the profitability of a company before taxes are taken into account. It is a useful tool for investors and analysts to assess the financial health of a company and compare it to other companies in the same industry. Pretax margin is also known as [[Operating Margin|operating margin]] or [[Operating Income|operating income]] margin.
===Definition of Pretax Margin===

Pretax margin is defined as the ratio of a company’s operating income to its total revenue. It is expressed as a percentage and is calculated by dividing the operating income by the total revenue. Operating income is the amount of money a company earns from its core business operations, before any taxes or other expenses are taken into account.
===Components of Pretax Margin===

The components of pretax margin include operating income, total revenue, and taxes. Operating income is the amount of money a company earns from its core business operations, before any taxes or other expenses are taken into account. Total revenue is the total amount of money a company earns from its operations, including sales, investments, and other sources. Taxes are the amount of money a company pays in taxes to the government.
===Calculating Pretax Margin===

Pretax margin is calculated by dividing the operating income by the total revenue. The formula for calculating pretax margin is:

Pretax Margin = Operating Income / Total Revenue

For example, if a company has an operating income of $100,000 and total revenue of $500,000, the pretax margin would be 20%.
===Benefits of Pretax Margin===

Pretax margin is a useful tool for investors and analysts to assess the financial health of a company and compare it to other companies in the same industry. It is also a good indicator of a company’s ability to generate profits before taxes are taken into account.
===Limitations of Pretax Margin===

Pretax margin does not take into account taxes, which can have a significant impact on a company’s profitability. Additionally, it does not take into account other expenses such as interest payments, which can also have an impact on a company’s profitability.
