===Introduction to Oligopoly===

Oligopoly is a market structure in which a few firms dominate the industry. It is characterized by a small number of firms that control the majority of the [[Market Share|market share]]. The firms in an oligopoly have a significant influence on the market price and output of the industry. Oligopolies are common in industries where there are high barriers to entry, such as the automobile industry, the airline industry, and the telecommunications industry.
===Characteristics of an Oligopoly===

Oligopolies are characterized by a few firms that dominate the industry. These firms have a significant influence on the market price and output of the industry. The firms in an oligopoly are interdependent, meaning that the decisions of one firm will affect the decisions of the other firms in the market. Additionally, oligopolies are characterized by high barriers to entry, which makes it difficult for new firms to enter the market.
===Examples of Oligopolies===

There are many examples of oligopolies in the modern economy. The automobile industry is an example of an oligopoly, as it is dominated by a few large firms such as [[General Motors|General Motors]], Ford, and Toyota. The airline industry is also an example of an oligopoly, as it is dominated by a few large firms such as American Airlines, Delta, and United. The telecommunications industry is another example of an oligopoly, as it is dominated by a few large firms such as AT&T, Verizon, and Sprint.
===Strategies Used in Oligopolies===

Firms in an oligopoly use various strategies to gain an advantage over their competitors. These strategies include price leadership, product differentiation, and non-price competition. Price leadership is when one firm sets the price for the industry, and the other firms in the market follow suit. Product differentiation is when firms differentiate their products in order to gain an advantage over their competitors. Non-price competition is when firms compete on factors other than price, such as advertising and [[Customer Service|customer service]].
===Impact of Oligopolies on the Economy===

Oligopolies can have both positive and negative impacts on the economy. On the one hand, oligopolies can lead to increased efficiency and innovation, as firms are incentivized to produce better products and services in order to gain an advantage over their competitors. On the other hand, oligopolies can lead to higher prices and reduced competition, which can lead to decreased consumer welfare.
===Conclusion===

In conclusion, oligopoly is a market structure in which a few firms dominate the industry. It is characterized by a small number of firms that control the majority of the market share, high barriers to entry, and interdependence among the firms.
