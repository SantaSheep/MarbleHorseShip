===Introduction to Marxism===
Marxism is a political and economic theory developed by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels in the 19th century. It is based on the idea that the world is divided into two classes: the bourgeoisie, or the owners of the means of production, and the proletariat, or the working class. Marxism seeks to explain the exploitation of the working class by the bourgeoisie and to provide a framework for a revolutionary transformation of society. Marxism is a form of socialism, and it has been influential in the development of many political and economic systems around the world.
===History of Marxism===
Marxism was developed by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels in the mid-19th century. Marx and Engels were both influenced by the ideas of the German philosopher Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel, who argued that history was a dialectical process in which one idea or system of thought was replaced by another. Marx and Engels applied this idea to the economic and political systems of their time, arguing that capitalism was a system of exploitation and that it would eventually be replaced by a more just and equitable system.
===Marxist Theory of Value===
Marxist theory of value is based on the idea that the value of a commodity is determined by the amount of labor that went into producing it. This is known as the labor theory of value. According to this theory, the value of a commodity is determined by the amount of labor that went into producing it, and not by the amount of money that was paid for it. This theory is used to explain why some commodities are more expensive than others, and why some people are able to make more money than others.
===Marxist Theory of Exploitation===
Marxist theory of exploitation is based on the idea that the working class is exploited by the bourgeoisie. ===Marxist Theory of Class Struggle===
Marxist theory of class struggle is based on the idea that the working class is oppressed by the bourgeoisie. According to this theory, the working class is forced to work for the bourgeoisie in order to survive, and the bourgeoisie are able to maintain their power and wealth by exploiting the working class. The Marxist theory of class struggle argues that the working class should organize and fight for their rights in order to overthrow the bourgeoisie and create a more just and equitable society.
