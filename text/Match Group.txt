===Introduction to Match Group===

Match Group is an American internet company that owns and operates several online dating websites and applications. It is the world’s largest provider of dating products, with a portfolio of over 45 brands, including Tinder, Match, PlentyOfFish, Meetic, OkCupid, OurTime, Pairs, and Hinge. Match Group is headquartered in Dallas, Texas, and is a subsidiary of IAC/InterActiveCorp. The company was founded in 2009 and went public in 2015.
===History of Match Group===

Match Group was founded in 2009 by entrepreneur and IAC/InterActiveCorp Chairman Barry Diller. The company was created to bring together IAC’s existing online dating businesses, which included Match.com, OkCupid, and PlentyOfFish. In 2011, Match Group acquired People Media, a provider of targeted online dating communities for singles over 50, and in 2014, it acquired Tinder, a location-based dating app. In 2015, Match Group went public on the NASDAQ stock exchange.
===Business Model of Match Group===

Match Group’s business model is based on providing a platform for users to connect with potential partners. The company’s portfolio of dating products includes both free and subscription-based services. The free services are supported by advertising, while the subscription-based services generate revenue through monthly subscription fees. Match Group also offers premium features, such as additional search filters and profile boosts, which are available for an additional fee.
