===Introduction to Hilton Worldwide===

Hilton Worldwide is a global hospitality company that owns, manages, and franchises a portfolio of hotels and resorts. The company was founded in 1919 by Conrad Hilton and is headquartered in McLean, Virginia. Hilton Worldwide operates more than 5,700 hotels and resorts in 109 countries and territories, with more than 890,000 rooms. The company has a presence in the luxury, full-service, select-service, extended-stay, and timeshare segments of the hospitality industry. Hilton Worldwide also offers a variety of services, such as loyalty programs, meeting and event planning, and vacation ownership.
===History of Hilton Worldwide===

Hilton Worldwide traces its roots back to 1919, when Conrad Hilton purchased his first hotel, the Mobley Hotel in Cisco, Texas. Over the next several decades, Hilton expanded his hotel empire, acquiring and building hotels in cities across the United States. In 1946, Hilton opened the first international hotel, the Caribe Hilton in San Juan, Puerto Rico. In the 1960s, Hilton began to expand its presence in Europe and Asia, and in the 1970s, the company began to focus on franchising and management contracts. In the 1980s, Hilton began to expand its portfolio of brands, launching the Hampton Inn and Suites brand in 1984. In the 1990s, Hilton acquired several hotel chains, including Promus Hotel Corporation and Scandic Hotels. In 2006, Hilton Hotels Corporation merged with The Blackstone Group to form Hilton Worldwide.
