===Introduction to Shareholder Value Added===

Shareholder [[Value Added|Value Added]] (SVA) is a financial metric used to measure the value created by a company for its shareholders. It is a measure of the [[Return On Investment|return on investment]] (ROI) that shareholders receive from their investments in the company. SVA is calculated by subtracting the [[Cost Of Capital|cost of capital]] from the company’s [[Operating Profit|operating profit]]. The cost of capital is the amount of money that the company must pay to borrow money or to issue equity. SVA is a useful metric for investors to evaluate the performance of a company and to compare it to other companies in the same industry.
===Definition of Shareholder Value Added===

Shareholder Value Added (SVA) is defined as the difference between the return on investment (ROI) and the cost of capital. It is a measure of the value created by a company for its shareholders. SVA is calculated by subtracting the cost of capital from the company’s operating profit. The cost of capital is the amount of money that the company must pay to borrow money or to issue equity.
