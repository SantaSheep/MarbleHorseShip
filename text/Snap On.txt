===History of Snap On===

Snap-on was founded in 1920 by Joseph Johnson and William Seidemann in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. Johnson and Seidemann had previously worked together at the Milwaukee Electric Tool Company, where they developed the first practical hand-held electric drill. After leaving Milwaukee Electric Tool, they founded Snap-on with the goal of creating a line of tools that could be used by professional mechanics. The company’s first product was a ratchet wrench, which was quickly followed by a line of other hand tools.

Snap-on quickly grew in popularity among professional mechanics, and by the mid-1930s the company had become a leader in the automotive tool industry. In the 1950s, Snap-on began to expand its [[Product Line|product line]] to include power tools, diagnostic equipment, and other specialized tools.
