===Introduction to Intel===

Intel Corporation is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Santa Clara, California. It is one of the world’s largest semiconductor chip makers and is the inventor of the x86 series of microprocessors, the processors found in most personal computers. Intel supplies processors for computer system manufacturers such as Apple, Lenovo, HP, and Dell. Intel also manufactures motherboard chipsets, network interface controllers and integrated circuits, flash memory, graphics chips, embedded processors and other devices related to communications and computing.
===History of Intel===

Intel was founded in 1968 by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore. The company’s name was derived from the words “integrated electronics”. Intel’s first product was the 3101 Schottky TTL bipolar 64-bit static random-access memory, which was released in 1969. In 1971, Intel released the first commercial microprocessor, the Intel 4004. This was followed by the Intel 8008 in 1972 and the Intel 8080 in 1974.
===Intel’s Business Model===

Intel’s business model is based on the design, manufacture, and sale of semiconductor chips and related products. Intel designs and manufactures a wide range of products, including microprocessors, chipsets, motherboards, graphics processors, and network interface controllers. Intel also provides software and services related to its products. Intel’s products are used in a variety of applications, including personal computers, servers, embedded systems, and mobile devices.
===Intel’s Financial Performance===

Intel has been a consistently profitable company since its founding. In 2019, Intel reported revenue of $71.9 billion and [[Net Income|net income]] of $21.1 billion. Intel’s [[Gross Margin|gross margin]] was 59.3%, and its [[Operating Margin|operating margin]] was 28.2%. Intel’s [[Return On Equity|return on equity]] was 24.2%, and its [[Return On Assets|return on assets]] was 15.2%. Intel’s stock price has increased significantly over the past five years, from $23.50 in 2014 to $60.50 in 2019.
===Intel’s Impact on the Technology Industry===

Intel has had a major impact on the technology industry. Intel’s microprocessors are used in a wide variety of products, from personal computers to servers to mobile devices. Intel’s chipsets and motherboards are used in many of the world’s most popular computers. Intel’s graphics processors are used in gaming consoles and high-end PCs. Intel’s network interface controllers are used in many networking products. Intel’s products have enabled the development of many new technologies, such as [[Cloud Computing|cloud computing]], artificial intelligence, and the Internet of Things.
