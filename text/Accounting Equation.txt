===Introduction to the Accounting Equation===
The accounting equation is a fundamental concept in accounting that states that the total assets of a business must equal the [[Total Liabilities|total liabilities]] plus the total equity of the business. This equation is used to measure the financial health of a business and is the basis for all [[Financial Statements|financial statements]]. It is also used to calculate the [[Net Worth|net worth]] of a business.
===Components of the Accounting Equation===
The accounting equation is composed of three components: assets, liabilities, and equity. Assets are the resources owned by a business, such as cash, inventory, and equipment. Liabilities are the debts and obligations of a business, such as loans and [[Accounts Payable|accounts payable]]. Equity is the difference between assets and liabilities, and is also known as the net worth of a business.
===Examples of the Accounting Equation===
The accounting equation can be expressed in the following formula: Assets = Liabilities + Equity. For example, if a business has $100,000 in assets, $50,000 in liabilities, and $50,000 in equity, the equation would be expressed as follows: $100,000 = $50,000 + $50,000.

The accounting equation is a useful tool, but it does have some limitations. For example, it does not take into account the value of [[In[[Tangible Asset|tangible Asset]]|intangible asset]]s, such as goodwill or intellectual property. Additionally, the equation does not take into account the value of future [[Cash Flow|cash flow]]s, which can be an important factor in determining the financial health of a business.
