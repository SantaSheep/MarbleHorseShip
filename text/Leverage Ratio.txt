===Introduction to Leverage Ratio === 
Leverage ratios are a type of financial metric used to measure the amount of debt a company has relative to its equity. Leverage ratios are used to assess a company’s financial health and to determine its ability to pay off its debt. Leverage ratios are also used to compare the financial performance of different companies.
===Types of Leverage Ratios === 
There are several different types of leverage ratios, including the debt-to-equity ratio, the debt-to-assets ratio, the debt-to-capital ratio, and the interest coverage ratio. The debt-to-equity ratio measures the amount of debt a company has relative to its equity. The debt-to-assets ratio measures the amount of debt a company has relative to its total assets. The debt-to-capital ratio measures the amount of debt a company has relative to its total capital. The interest coverage ratio measures the ability of a company to pay its interest expenses.
===Calculating Leverage Ratios === 
Leverage ratios are calculated by dividing a company’s total debt by its total equity, total assets, total capital, or interest expenses. For example, the debt-to-equity ratio is calculated by dividing a company’s total debt by its total equity. The debt-to-assets ratio is calculated by dividing a company’s total debt by its total assets. The debt-to-capital ratio is calculated by dividing a company’s total debt by its total capital. The interest coverage ratio is calculated by dividing a company’s total interest expenses by its total income.
===Benefits of Leverage Ratios === 
Leverage ratios are beneficial for investors and creditors because they provide an indication of a company’s financial health and its ability to pay off its debt. Leverage ratios can also be used to compare the financial performance of different companies.
===Risks of Leverage Ratios === 
The main risk associated with leverage ratios is that they can be misleading if a company has a large amount of debt but a small amount of equity. This can lead to an artificially high leverage ratio, which can give investors and creditors a false sense of security.
