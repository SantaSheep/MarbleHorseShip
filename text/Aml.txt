===Introduction to AML===

Anti-[[Money Laundering|Money Laundering]] (AML) is a set of laws, regulations, and procedures designed to prevent criminals from disguising illegally obtained funds as legitimate income. It is a global effort to combat money laundering and terrorist financing. Money laundering is the process of disguising the source of illegally obtained funds by transferring them through a series of transactions. It is a major concern for governments and [[Financial Institution|financial institution]]s around the world, as it can be used to finance terrorism, organized crime, and other illegal activities.
===Regulatory Framework for AML===

The regulatory framework for AML is established by the Financial Action Task Force (FATF). The FATF is an intergovernmental body that sets standards and promotes effective implementation of legal, regulatory, and operational measures for combating money laundering, terrorist financing, and other related threats to the integrity of the international [[Financial System|financial system]]. The FATF has developed a set of 40 Recommendations that provide a comprehensive framework for AML compliance. These Recommendations are regularly updated to reflect changes in the global financial system and to ensure that AML measures remain effective.
===AML Compliance Programs===

Financial institutions are required to establish and maintain an effective AML compliance program. This program should include policies, procedures, and controls that are designed to detect and prevent money laundering activities. The program should also include customer [[Due Diligence|due diligence]] measures, such as customer identification and verification, and ongoing monitoring of customer transactions. Financial institutions should also have a system in place to report suspicious activities to the appropriate authorities.

===AML Monitoring and Reporting===

Financial institutions should have a system in place to monitor customer transactions for suspicious activity. This system should include automated transaction monitoring systems, as well as manual reviews of customer transactions. Financial institutions should also have a system in place to report suspicious activities to the appropriate authorities.
