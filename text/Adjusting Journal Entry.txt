===Types of Adjusting Journal Entries === 
Examples of Adjusting Journal Entries - Examples of adjusting journal entries include recording accrued salaries, recording prepaid expenses, recording unearned revenues, recording depreciation, recording amortization, and recording bad debts.

===Benefits of Adjusting Journal Entries === 
Conclusion - Adjusting journal entries are an important part of the accounting process. They help to ensure that the company’s financial statements accurately reflect the company’s financial position. However, they can be difficult to understand and can be time-consuming to prepare. It is important to ensure that the entries are properly recorded in order to ensure accuracy.
