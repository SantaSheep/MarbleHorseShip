===Introduction to Per Capita GDP===
[[Per Capita|Per capita]] GDP is a measure of a country’s economic output per person. It is used to compare the economic output of different countries and to measure the economic progress of a country over time. Per capita GDP is calculated by dividing a country’s gross domestic product (GDP) by its population.
===Definition of Per Capita GDP===
Per capita GDP is a measure of a country’s economic output per person. It is calculated by dividing a country’s gross domestic product (GDP) by its population. It is used to compare the economic output of different countries and to measure the economic progress of a country over time.
===Components of Per Capita GDP===
Per capita GDP is composed of two components: the total output of goods and services produced in a country and the population of the country. The total output of goods and services produced in a country is measured by the country’s gross domestic product (GDP). The population of the country is measured by the number of people living in the country.
===Factors Affecting Per Capita GDP===
There are several factors that can affect a country’s per capita GDP. These include the size of the population, the level of economic development, the level of technology, the level of education, the level of investment, and the level of government spending.
===Uses of Per Capita GDP===
Per capita GDP is used to compare the economic output of different countries and to measure the economic progress of a country over time. It is also used to compare the economic output of different regions within a country. It can also be used to compare the economic output of different industries within a country.
