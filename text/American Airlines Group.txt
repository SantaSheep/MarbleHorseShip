===Introduction to American Airlines Group===

American Airlines Group (AAG) is a major American airline [[Holding Company|holding company]] headquartered in Fort Worth, Texas. It is the world’s largest airline when measured by fleet size, revenue, scheduled passengers carried, and number of destinations served. AAG operates a mainline fleet of over 900 aircraft, with hubs in Charlotte, Dallas/Fort Worth, Los Angeles, Miami, New York, Philadelphia, Phoenix, and Washington,  It also operates regional service under the American Eagle brand.
===History of American Airlines Group===

AAG was formed in 2013 following the merger of American Airlines and US Airways. The merger created the world’s largest airline, with a fleet of over 900 aircraft and hubs in nine major cities. The merger was approved by the U.S. Department of Justice in 2013, and the two airlines officially merged on December 9, 2013.
===Financial Performance of American Airlines Group===

AAG has seen strong [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] in recent years. In 2019, the company reported total revenue of $44.6 billion, up from $42.2 billion in 2018. [[Operating Income|Operating income]] was $3.9 billion in 2019, up from $3.3 billion in 2018. [[Net Income|Net income]] was $2.7 billion in 2019, up from $2.2 billion in 2018. The company’s total assets were $51.2 billion in 2019, up from $48.2 billion in 2018.
===Management of American Airlines Group===

AAG is led by Chairman and CEO Doug Parker. He is supported by a team of experienced executives, including President Robert Isom, Chief Financial Officer Derek Kerr, and Chief Operating Officer Robert Isom. The company also has a [[Board Of Directors|Board of Directors]], which is chaired by former U.S. Secretary of Transportation Ray LaHood.
