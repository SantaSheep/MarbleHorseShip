===Introduction to Market Capitalization-to-GDP Ratio === 
The market capitalization-to-GDP ratio is a measure of the size of a country’s stock market relative to its gross domestic product (GDP). It is used to compare the size of a country’s stock market to its economy and to compare the size of different countries’ stock markets. This ratio is also known as the “market cap-to-GDP ratio” or “market cap-to-GDP ratio.”
===What is Market Capitalization-to-GDP Ratio? === 
The market capitalization-to-GDP ratio is a measure of the size of a country’s stock market relative to its gross domestic product (GDP). It is calculated by dividing the total market capitalization of all publicly traded companies in a country by the country’s GDP. The ratio is expressed as a percentage. A higher ratio indicates that the stock market is larger relative to the size of the economy.
===How to Calculate Market Capitalization-to-GDP Ratio === 
The market capitalization-to-GDP ratio is calculated by dividing the total market capitalization of all publicly traded companies in a country by the country’s GDP. The market capitalization of a company is the total value of its outstanding shares. It is calculated by multiplying the number of outstanding shares by the current market price of the stock. The GDP of a country is the total value of all goods and services produced in the country in a given year.
===Benefits of Market Capitalization-to-GDP Ratio === 
The market capitalization-to-GDP ratio is a useful tool for comparing the size of different countries’ stock markets. It can also be used to compare the size of a country’s stock market to its economy. A higher ratio indicates that the stock market is larger relative to the size of the economy. This can be used to identify countries with large and growing stock markets.
===Limitations of Market Capitalization-to-GDP Ratio === 
The market capitalization-to-GDP ratio is not a perfect measure of the size of a country’s stock market. It does not take into account the size of the companies in the stock market or the liquidity of the stocks. It also does not take into account the size of the economy outside of the stock market.
