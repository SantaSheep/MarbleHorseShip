===Introduction to IRAs === 
An IRA, or Individual Retirement Account, is a type of retirement savings account that allows individuals to save for retirement in a tax-advantaged way. IRAs are a great way to save for retirement, as they offer tax benefits, flexibility, and a variety of investment options.
===Types of IRAs === 
There are several types of IRAs available, each with its own set of rules and benefits. The most common types of IRAs are Traditional IRAs, Roth IRAs, SEP IRAs, and SIMPLE IRAs.
===Traditional IRA === 
A Traditional IRA is a retirement savings account that allows individuals to save for retirement on a tax-deferred basis. Contributions to a Traditional IRA are tax-deductible, and earnings grow tax-deferred until withdrawn.
===Roth IRA === 
A Roth IRA is a retirement savings account that allows individuals to save for retirement on a tax-free basis. Contributions to a Roth IRA are not tax-deductible, but earnings grow tax-free until withdrawn.
===SEP IRA === 
A SEP IRA is a retirement savings account that allows employers to make contributions on behalf of their employees. Contributions to a SEP IRA are tax-deductible, and earnings grow tax-deferred until withdrawn.
===SIMPLE IRA === 
A SIMPLE IRA is a retirement savings account that allows employers to make contributions on behalf of their employees. Contributions to a SIMPLE IRA are tax-deductible, and earnings grow tax-deferred until withdrawn.
===Benefits of IRAs === 
IRAs offer a variety of benefits, including tax advantages, flexibility, and a variety of investment options. IRAs are also a great way to save for retirement, as they allow individuals to save for retirement in a tax-advantaged way.
===Contribution Limits === 
The contribution limits for IRAs vary depending on the type of IRA. For example, the contribution limit for a Traditional IRA is $6,000 per year, while the contribution limit for a Roth IRA is $6,000 per year.
===Withdrawal Rules === 
The withdrawal rules for IRAs vary depending on the type of IRA. For example, withdrawals from a Traditional IRA are subject to income tax, while withdrawals from a Roth IRA are not subject to income tax.
