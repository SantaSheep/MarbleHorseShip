===Introduction to Debt-Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) === 
Debt-Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) is a financial metric used to measure a company’s ability to pay its debt obligations. It is a key indicator of a company’s financial health and is used by lenders to assess the risk of lending to a company. DSCR is a ratio of a company’s net operating income to its total debt service obligations. A higher DSCR indicates that a company has a greater ability to pay its debt obligations.
