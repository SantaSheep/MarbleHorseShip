===Introduction to Freecashflow===

Freecashflow is a financial term used to describe the amount of cash that a company has available to use for investments, dividends, and other activities. It is an important measure of a company's financial health and is used to assess the company's ability to generate [[Cash Flow|cash flow]] from its operations. Freecashflow is calculated by subtracting [[Capital Expenditure|capital expenditure]]s from [[Operating Cash Flow|operating cash flow]]. It is a key indicator of a company's [[Financial Performance|financial performance]] and is used to assess the company's ability to generate cash flow from its operations.
===Definition of Freecashflow===

Freecashflow is defined as the amount of cash that a company has available to use for investments, dividends, and other activities. ===Components of Freecashflow===

The components of freecashflow include operating cash flow, capital expenditures, and other activities. Operating cash flow is the amount of cash generated from a company's operations.
