===Introduction to PayPal===

PayPal is a digital payments platform that enables individuals and businesses to send and receive money online. It is one of the most popular payment methods in the world, with over 305 million active users and over 17 million merchants. PayPal is a subsidiary of eBay, and is headquartered in San Jose, California. PayPal offers a variety of services, including online payments, money transfers, and merchant services. PayPal also offers a range of financial services, such as credit and [[Debit Card|debit card]]s, and a mobile wallet.
===History of PayPal===

PayPal was founded in 1998 by Peter Thiel, Max Levchin, and Elon Musk. The company was initially called Confinity, and was focused on providing secure money transfers for Palm Pilot users. In 2000, Confinity merged with com, an online banking company founded by Elon Musk. The merged company was renamed PayPal, and the focus shifted to providing online payments. In 2002, PayPal was acquired by eBay for $1.5 billion. Since then, PayPal has grown to become one of the world’s largest digital payments platforms.
===PayPal’s Business Model===

PayPal’s business model is based on providing a secure and convenient way for individuals and businesses to send and receive money online. PayPal charges a fee for each transaction, which is typically a percentage of the transaction amount. PayPal also offers a range of financial services, such as credit and debit cards, and a mobile wallet.
