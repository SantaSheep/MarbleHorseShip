===Introduction to Return on Average Capital Employed (ROACE)===

Return on Average [[Capital Employed|Capital Employed]] (ROACE) is a financial metric used to measure the profitability of a company. It is calculated by dividing the company’s net [[Operating Profit|operating profit]] after taxes (NOPAT) by its average capital employed (ACE). ROACE is a useful metric for investors and analysts to assess the efficiency of a company’s management in generating profits from its capital investments.
===Calculation of ROACE===

ROACE is calculated by dividing the company’s NOPAT by its ACE. NOPAT is calculated by subtracting the company’s operating expenses from its [[Operating Income|operating income]]. ACE is calculated by adding the company’s total assets and [[Total Liabilities|total liabilities]] and then dividing by two. The resulting number is the company’s average capital employed.
===Advantages of ROACE===

ROACE is a useful metric for investors and analysts to assess the efficiency of a company’s management in generating profits from its capital investments. It is also a good indicator of a company’s ability to generate returns on its investments. ROACE can be used to compare the performance of different companies in the same industry.
===Disadvantages of ROACE===

ROACE does not take into account the company’s debt levels, which can have a significant impact on the company’s profitability. Additionally, ROACE does not take into account the company’s [[Cash Flow|cash flow]], which can also have an impact on the company’s profitability.
