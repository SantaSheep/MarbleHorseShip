===Introduction to Money Market Funds===

[[Money Market|Money market]] funds are a type of [[Mutual Fund|mutual fund]] that invests in short-term [[Debt Instrument|debt instrument]]s such as [[Treasury Bill|Treasury bill]]s, certificates of deposit, and [[Commercial Paper|commercial paper]]. Money market funds are designed to provide investors with a safe and liquid investment option that offers a higher [[Rate Of Return|rate of return]] than a traditional savings account. Money market funds are regulated by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and are subject to certain restrictions.
===Types of Money Market Funds===

There are two main types of money market funds: taxable and tax-exempt. Taxable money market funds invest in short-term debt instruments that are subject to federal [[Income Tax|income tax]]es. Tax-exempt money market funds invest in short-term debt instruments that are exempt from federal income taxes.
===Advantages of Money Market Funds===

Money market funds offer several advantages to investors. They are relatively safe investments, as they are subject to SEC regulations and are diversified across a variety of short-term debt instruments. Money market funds also offer a higher rate of return than a traditional savings account, making them an attractive option for investors looking to maximize their returns.
