===Introduction to Inflation Accounting === 
Inflation accounting is a method of accounting that takes into account the effects of inflation on the financial statements of a company. It is used to adjust the financial statements of a company to reflect the true economic value of the company’s assets and liabilities. Inflation accounting is used to provide a more accurate picture of a company’s financial position and performance.
===Types of Inflation Accounting === 
There are two main types of inflation accounting: current cost accounting and constant purchasing power accounting. Current cost accounting is used to adjust the financial statements of a company to reflect the current cost of goods and services. Constant purchasing power accounting is used to adjust the financial statements of a company to reflect the purchasing power of money over time.
