===Introduction to Inflation Accounting===
Inflati[[On Account|on account]]ing is a method of accounting that takes into account the effects of inflation on the [[Financial Statements|financial statements]] of a company. It is used to adjust the financial statements of a company to reflect the true economic value of the company’s assets and liabilities. Inflation accounting is used to provide a more accurate picture of a company’s financial position and performance.
===History of Inflation Accounting===
Inflation accounting has been around since the early 1900s, when it was first used to adjust the financial statements of companies to reflect the effects of inflation. In the 1950s, the International [[Accounting Standard|Accounting Standard]]s Board (IASB) began to develop a set of standards for inflation accounting. These standards were adopted by many countries and are still in use today.
===Types of Inflation Accounting===
There are two main types of inflation accounting: current [[Cos[[T Account|t Account]]ing|cost accounting]] and [[Historical Cost|historical cost]] accounting. Current cost accounting is used to adjust the financial statements of a company to reflect the current value of its assets and liabilities. Historical cost accounting is used to adjust the financial statements of a company to reflect the original cost of its assets and liabilities.
