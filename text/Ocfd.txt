===Introduction to Ocfd===

Operating [[Cash Flow|Cash Flow]] to Debt (OCFD) is a financial ratio used to measure a company’s ability to pay off its debt obligations. It is calculated by dividing a company’s [[Operating Cash Flow|operating cash flow]] by its total debt. This ratio is used to assess a company’s financial health and its ability to meet its debt obligations.
===Definition of Ocfd===

Operating Cash Flow to Debt (OCFD) is a financial ratio that measures a company’s ability to pay off its debt obligations. It is calculated by dividing a company’s operating cash flow by its total debt. This ratio is used to assess a company’s financial health and its ability to meet its debt obligations.

The concept of Operating Cash Flow to Debt (OCFD) was first introduced in the early 1980s by financial analysts as a way to measure a company’s ability to pay off its debt obligations.
