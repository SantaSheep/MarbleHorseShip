===Introduction to Occurrence Policy===

An occurrence policy is a type of insurance policy that provides coverage for losses that occur during the policy period, regardless of when the claim is made. This type of policy is typically used to protect businesses from losses that may occur due to unforeseen circumstances. It is important to note that an occurrence policy does not cover losses that occur after the policy period has ended.
===Types of Occurrence Policies===

There are several different types of occurrence policies available. The most common type is a general liability policy, which provides coverage for bodily injury, property damage, and personal and advertising injury. Other types of occurrence policies include professional liability, product liability, and errors and omissions policies.
===Benefits of Occurrence Policies===

One of the main benefits of an occurrence policy is that it provides coverage for losses that occur during the policy period, regardless of when the claim is made. This means that businesses can be protected from losses that may occur due to unforeseen circumstances. Additionally, occurrence policies typically provide broader coverage than other types of policies, such as claims-made policies.
===Drawbacks of Occurrence Policies===

One of the main drawbacks of an occurrence policy is that it does not provide coverage for losses that occur after the policy period has ended. This means that businesses may be exposed to losses that occur after the policy period has ended. Additionally, occurrence policies typically have higher premiums than other types of policies, such as claims-made policies.
