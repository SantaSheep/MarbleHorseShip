===Introduction to Q Ratio (Tobin's Q) === 
Q Ratio, also known as Tobin's Q, is a financial metric used to measure the market value of a company relative to its book value. It is a measure of the company's financial health and can be used to compare the performance of different companies. The Q Ratio is an important tool for investors and analysts to assess the value of a company and its stock.
===What is Q Ratio (Tobin's Q)? === 
The Q Ratio, also known as Tobin's Q, is a financial metric used to measure the market value of a company relative to its book value. It is calculated by dividing the market value of a company's assets by the book value of its assets. The Q Ratio is an important tool for investors and analysts to assess the value of a company and its stock.
===How to Calculate Q Ratio (Tobin's Q) === 
The Q Ratio is calculated by dividing the market value of a company's assets by the book value of its assets. The market value of a company's assets is calculated by taking the current market price of the company's stock and multiplying it by the number of outstanding shares. The book value of a company's assets is calculated by taking the total assets of the company and subtracting any liabilities.
