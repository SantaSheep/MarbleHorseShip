===Introduction to Cardinal Health===
Cardinal Health is a global healthcare services and products company that provides medical products, services, and solutions to hospitals, health systems, pharmacies, ambulatory surgery centers, clinical laboratories, and physician offices worldwide. The company was founded in 1971 and is headquartered in Dublin, Ohio. Cardinal Health operates in two segments: Pharmaceutical and Medical. The Pharmaceutical segment distributes branded and generic pharmaceuticals, over-the-counter healthcare products, and other related products to retail and institutional customers. The Medical segment provides medical, surgical, and laboratory products, as well as services to hospitals, ambulatory surgery centers, clinical laboratories, and physician offices.
===History of Cardinal Health===
Cardinal Health was founded in 1971 by Robert  Walter and Robert  Walter Jr. as Cardinal Foods, a regional food distributor in the Midwest. In 1979, the company changed its name to Cardinal Health and began to focus on the healthcare industry. In the 1980s, the company began to expand its operations and acquired several companies, including the American Hospital Supply Corporation, which was the largest healthcare supply company in the United States at the time. In the 1990s, Cardinal Health continued to expand its operations and acquired several more companies, including the Pyxis Corporation, which was a leading provider of automated medication dispensing systems.
===Business Model of Cardinal Health===
Cardinal Health’s business model is focused on providing healthcare services and products to hospitals, health systems, pharmacies, ambulatory surgery centers, clinical laboratories, and physician offices. The company’s core services include pharmaceutical distribution, medical products distribution, and healthcare technology solutions. The company’s pharmaceutical distribution services include the distribution of branded and generic pharmaceuticals, over-the-counter healthcare products, and other related products to retail and institutional customers. The company’s medical products distribution services include the distribution of medical, surgical, and laboratory products, as well as services to hospitals, ambulatory surgery centers, clinical laboratories, and physician offices. The company’s healthcare technology solutions include the development and implementation of automated medication dispensing systems, medication management systems, and other healthcare technology solutions.
